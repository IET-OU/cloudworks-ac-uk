The 2017 FutureLearn Academic Network (FLAN) day at The Open University takes place on Friday 16 June (9am-4pm BST). It is open to anyone employed by a FutureLearn partner organisation.

The day is hosted by the Computers and Learning Research Group (CALRG) at The Open University and forms the final day of the group's three-day annual conference.
There's a blog post about the event here: MOOCs – An Awkward Dance

You can watch the presentations on the IET YouTube site.

09.00-09.30

Coffee available

09.30-09.40

Eileen Scanlon (OU) – Opening remarks

09.40-10.50

FLAN presentations: teaching and researching in MOOCs

  • Tina Papathoma (OU) – Who are the MOOC educators and what are their tasks? A multiple case study
  • Fereshte Goshtasbpour (Southampton) – What do educators’ contributions to MOOC discussion areas look like?
  • Shi Min Chua (OU) – Investigating dialogic learning and teaching in MOOCs: an applied linguistics approach
  • Alison Fox, Jim Askham and Rachel Tunstall (OU and Warwick) High responsibility: The ethics of researching a MOOC about research ethics (lightning presentation)

10.50-11.00

Short coffee break

11.00-11.45

FLAN presentations: Analysing MOOCs (1)

  • Ahmed Al-Imarah (Bath) ­ Quality assurance and innovation: case studies of massive open online courses in UK Higher Education
  • Steve Cayzer and Simon Coton (Bath) – Social network analysis of learning: application of significance tests to massive open online courses (MOOCs)
  • Philip Tubman, Phillip Benachour and Murat Oztok (Lancaster) – Visualising social learning for discoverability (lightning presentation)

11.45-12.15

FLAN presentations: MOOC accessibility

  • Francisco Iniesto, Patrick McAndrew, Shailey Minocha and Tim Coughlan (OU) – What are the expectations of disabled learners when participating in a MOOC?
  • Garron Hillaire, Francisco Iniesto and Bart Rienties (OU)– The roadmap to emotionally accessible MOOCs

12.15-13.00

Lunch

13.00-14.00

FLAN presentations: Analysing MOOCs (2)

  • Hillary E. Merzdorf, Nathan M. Hicks and Kerrie A. Douglas (Purdue) – Psychometric analyses of the expectancy-value-cost scale in advanced nanotechnology MOOCs
  • Tim O’Riordan (Southampton) – Automated evaluation of comments in a MOOC discussion forum
  • Janesh Sanzgiri (OU) – A comparison of Indian learner experiences on a local and global MOOC platform

14.00-14.30

Coffee

14.30-15.30

FLAN presentations: Next steps

  • Eileen Scanlon (OU) – Recent trends in MOOC research: from three US conferences
  • Mirjam Hauck (OU) – How to design for transition: beyond MOOCs
  • James Little (Sheffield) – Beyond MOOCs: a catalyst for change

15.30-16.00

Discussant – Professor Rupert Wegerif, University of Cambridge
