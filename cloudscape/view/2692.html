MOOCH has many meanings. How many of these will be relevant over the next few weeks?

MOOCH

  1. to borrow (a small item or amount) without intending to return or repay it.
  2. to get or take wihtout paying or at another's expense; sponge: He always mooches cigarettes
  3. to beg
  4. to steal (verb used without object)
  5. to skulk or sneak
  6. to loiter or wander about
(Dictionary.com)
I intended meaning 6 in my title - but maybe some of the others are equally appropriate, especially in relation to OER?
