Slide 1:  Is it time to change?

Is it time to make the move from traditional classroom training to something new?

Is it time to put our flipcharts and other traditional training methods back in the box?

Slide 2: Moving to open practice.

If we move to open practice we can share our knowledge openly with our colleagues and also our extended network.

Using online tools such as Linkedin, Twitter and YouTube we can create a learning network where we can help others who in turn are able to help us.

Slide 3: But why should I share my knowledge?

But why should I share my knowledge?  I’m not sure that I want to.

Slide 4: Lets Explore Motivation.

So, lets explore motivation and find out about ‘What’s in it for me?’

Some people are motivated by factors such as achievement and others by factors such as money.

Motivation is different for everyone.

Slide 5: How do I work in this new way?

OK, so now I see – I want to share and be open but how do I work in this new way?

Slide 6: We need to learn how to use new tools.

I’ve got an idea.  We need to learn how to use a selection of new tools to help us to share our knowledge online. 

Slide 7: Come and find out more at the conference.

Come and find out more about the creation of a multimedia presentation designed to help innovate workplace learning.

Presentation title: From ‘Why should I?’ to ‘How could I? Innovating learning in the workplace through the implementation of open practice.

Date: February 9th 2015.

Location: Open University H818 online conference.

Presented by: Catherine Dartnall.
