Teresa Morgan (Terri Rees group) 4SPICEs and e-design models

  • Favourite
  • 427 views

Cloud created by:

Teresa Morgan
28 March 2014

Having looked at the 4SPPICEs model, I thought the idea of considering the teacher and technician as separate may have been helpful (here I’m considering a secondary school where often technology has to be requested via an IT technican and is not always available).  4SPPICES also helped to consider when different things were happening and break up the design into Space, Pedagogical method, Participants and History.  However I cannot imagine using this as a way of designing learning.  The elements felt too separated and I didn’t think it was therefore easy to read the flow of the design.

I was considering lesson plans I have used in the past which have columns highlighting what teachers are doing, what pupils are doing, the equipment that is being used and also the objective throughout the lesson.  As you can see what everyone needs at a given point in the lesson I feel it is much easier to have an overall feel for the lesson than the design 4SPPIces model.

 

e-Design Template

When I looked at this I thought it made you consider how the learner moved through the different stages from being very much teacher managed through to a self organised learner, which was useful.

The column system was an easy way to see how time was split between learner and teacher (but didn’t add that much to lesson plans, as in the past the format I have used a column for learner and then teacher so it’s easy to see who is doing what).  I did wonder if having the activity blocked in colour to highlight the amount of teacher led and student led activity would help focus the amount of time spent by each set on an activity. 

I did however think it was a useful way to concentrate the mind on moving students from teacher managed through to self-organised learner in an overarching scheme of work.

I personally preferred this layout to the 4SPPICES model, but this may have been because it was closer to the lesson plans I am used to.

Teresa
