Diane Goodman: dreaming of designing a learner-centred module for a learning & teaching course in a Moodle online environment

  • Favourite
  • 666 views

Cloud created by:

Diane Goodman
27 January 2013

Context

I am an academic developer and coordinate a university learning and teaching course. I am in the process of facilitating change in terms of curriculum design, learning design and the online learning environment, at a time when the whole institution is navigating infrastructure changes. Academics newly appointed to teaching, or to the university, are required to undertake the course and it is essential that the learning design ‘walks the talk’ - models reflexive pedagogy, in terms of the breadth of pedagogy incorporated into the course modules, effective learning design and authentic learning and teaching strategies.

I recently participated in Grainne Conole’s 7Cs Learning Design workshop and have begun incorporating and adapting aspects (such as the course feature cards and the Carpe Diem Resource Audit) into the development process. I am collaborating with a small team throughout this process and will scaffold learning activities and tasks in Moodle, as each module is redeveloped. The tricky part is avoiding the notion of ‘transition’ - module components really need to be designed from the ground up, according to the desired pedagogy and course features to be included. The course learning outcomes emphasise the critical relationship between theory and practice, and with this in mind, the outcomes of several major research projects provide valuable resources and ideas for the redevelopment process. Amongst others are:

  • The ALTC Professional Development Unit, Effective Teaching, Effective Learning in the Quantitative Disciplines aims to provide “national discipline specific” professional development for university lecturers and tutors teaching in the mathematical sciences. The project was developed in conjunction with the Australian Mathematical Society (AMS), and in consultation with a reference group representing the Statistical Society of Australia, Australian Association of Mathematics Teachers, Australian Mathematical Sciences Institute, Australian Science Deans Council, the Australian Business Deans Council and Engineers Australia. The 12 online Modules include “practical, discipline-specific and best-practice strategies for teaching and assessment so as to enhance student engagement and learning in mathematics”.
  • CADAD (Council of Australian directors of Academic Development) University Teacher Preparations Program Online Workshop: this workshop supports those who are working in teaching and learning centres or departments within universities and who are teaching in UTPPs for the first time.

Some of the key areas of focus for my dream...

  • constructive alignment of learning outcomes
  • authentic learning & assessment
  • learner-centrednes
  • communities of practice: peer-assisted learning and collaboration
  • reflective practice
  • criterion-referenced assessment
  • feedback and feed-forward
  • deep learning

What I am seeking ...

I hope to create a workflow and design framework for the module development process. And complete at least one module! 

Design framework =  curriculum - learning design - online learning environment (moodle resources and activities)

How I will do it ...

Collaborative governance re the collaborative redesign of modules: initial staff planning workshops were designed around a collaborative governance model 

Consider:

  • Reflexive pedagogy
  • Constructivist theory
  • Social constructivist theory
  • Phenomenological theory
  • Deep learning

The design of course modules will vary according to the specific curriculum and pedagogical focus, and this will determine the learning design and scaffolding of online learning activities and tasks, for each module.

I am really keen to develop new networks and collaborative partnerships in this field ...
