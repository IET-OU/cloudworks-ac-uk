EMOOCs 2014, the Second MOOC European Stakeholders Summit, will be held on February 10-12, 2014 in Lausanne (Switzerland).

Submissions deadline:  September 20, 2013

Organised by the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) and P.A.U. Education the event aims to be an opportunity to gather European actors involved in the Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) phenomenon, from policy makers practitioners and researchers. This conference is the follow-up of the MOOC Summit 2013.

The goal of the summit is to develop synergies among European universities around themes such as student assessment, MOOC accreditation, platform interoperability or joint research initiatives.

The conference will include four tracks (Policy, Experience, Research and Business) that are explained in detail below, with 5 sessions of 90 minutes and a keynote speaker. EMOOCs 2014 will also offer a series of workshops, meetings and the tutorial “All you need to know about MOOCs”.

I understand that the 'experiences' track for the EMOOC conference has left two tracks open to see 'emerging' topics.  Great idea for a conference around MOOCs. What stream do we see emerging for our proposal?

  • Participant Experiences

I also like the learning as a change in practise.  Oldsmooc definitely influenced how I move through and understand online learning/teaching.  Is this focus too specific to the content of Oldsmooc?  Or is that part of looking at the stories from the trenchs for Oldsmooc participants?

Here are some of the things that have changed for me:

  • MOOC forced use/engagement in variety of spaces (Twitter, cloudworks, Bibsonomy, youtube, G+, etc; developed as a user of these tools, started to develop habits/pathways to tools for specific purposes; choices/preferences for the tools for different need/activity.  There was alot of 'squawking' at the beginning from participants about the diversity of place but I feel as a participant VITAL skills to have.
  • global network of professionals to learn from (directionality of interaction for me is in following 'more capable' peers, experts); but I think that having these networks is growing the knowledge of those that 'follow me' (have no proof of this though:)  However, I believe since "Living the Dream" of Oldsmooc, I am much more 'collegial' and attempt have others indulge in the learning opportunities available online.  The Oldsmooc team in essence modeled a way of being and learning online.  I appreciated the 'behind the scenes' revelations (on you YouTube vids).  I  have much more openess to opening the veil and bringing people into the discussion.  I have regular webinars for/with other directors.  I share all of my organizational templates in a local repository.  I moderate a professional forum.  etc.
  •  renewed motivation to more closely align teaching with research/theory; implementation of personas within our organizational context; use of OULDI cards with staff (just two weeks ago) to plan for content development; more internal research on badging/establishing criteria understanding how it will work within our context; the inclusion of CC licences on materials and inclusion of OER resources; tapping into local professional expertise for OER development; reinvestigation of organization 'evaluation' of delivering outcomes (Work in Progress)
  • confidence to learn/participate in a discussion within a HE context; applied to do a Masters (yes, started this term).

Ok that is the top of my head, and off the side of my desk if you will!

Briar
