The reality of the knowledge society

  • Favourite
  • 1034 views

Angela MacFarlane talk at CAL 09 conference, Brighton 09

Cloud created by:

Gráinne Conole
23 March 2009

Angela reflected on some of the rhetoric around the knowledge society. She considered two inter-related aspects:
•    Implications of employability
•    Implications for education?

She drew on work from Voogt and Pelgrum (2005) pedagogy in knowledge-based society.


She argued there is a change from pedagogy in an industrial society to pedagogy in the information society.

  1. Active learners (from activities prescribed by teacher to determined by the learner),
  2. Collaborative (individual to working in teams),
  3. Creative (reproductive learning to productive learning),
  4. Integrative (no link bet theory and practice to integrative approach),
  5. Evaluative (teacher-directed and summative to student-directed and diagnostic)

Potential of digital technologies to support learning show three powerful trends:
1.    Interaction of people and technologies
2.    Production (iteration?)
3.    Feedback and play

From some of her research studies Angela summarised the following in terms of kids use of PDAs.

High users

  • Relish and enjoy use of the PDA, are proud of owning a PDA
  • Are confident and active in developing their skills using the device
  • Appreciate the potential of the device and seek to use it
  • Explore what the device can do

Low users

  • Have basic level of skill and understanding of the PDA,
  • Not pro-active, are happy to be dependent on others,
  • Have inaccurate perceptions about provision, systems and what the PDA can do,
  • Have little exposure to use in the classrom
