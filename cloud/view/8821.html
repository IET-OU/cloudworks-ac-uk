iCloud Keychain: Apple’s Cloud-based Password Manager

  • Favourite
  • 1341 views

Cloud created by:

Kyle H. Albert
27 October 2014

 

Since Macintosh OS X Mavericks and iOS 7.0.3, Apple integrated cloud-based feature called the iCloud Keychain, a free password and account manager. With this iCloud add-on, your user names, passwords, Wi-Fi network log-ins, and credit card information can now be safely stored in a virtual storage.   Let’s take a look at how the innovative technology works and why it is a major leap in revolutionizing the future of cloud computing.  

Enhancing The Cloud Experience   Since it was first introduced in 2011, Apple’s proprietary cloud storage basically acts as a typical backup and recovery system for iOS devices and Mac computers. As depicted at the iPhone 6 on O2 page, it lets you back up all your documents, contacts, and images stored on your iPhone and “share them across all your other devices,”under the same Apple ID. With the inclusion of Keychain, the Cupertino Company is also giving their users the opportunity to store and share their online log-in details (passwords and email addresses), credit card data, and even Wi-Fi passwords on iCloud -- bringing an enhanced experience and convenience to its users.  

As Password Manager   It does a great job of automatically entering passwords in auto-fill websites using the built-in Safari browser. The moment you visit a URL (i.e. Facebook or Twitter),Safari will prompt you if you want your log-in details to be remembered. It will automatically store information in an inconvenient location on your iOS/Mac, making it hard to find, access, and copy. Then, whenever you use your Mac or iPhone, the same log-in info will be suggested once you visit the same URL with Safari.   How To Set It Up   There are two ways on how you can configure the iCloud Keychain: using your PC or your device.  

Let’s start first with the Mac OS X Mavericks or later:  

  • Go to the iCloud pane located under the Mac’s System Preferences.
  • Check the Keychain option and re-enter your Apple ID.
  • You will be prompted to create your cloud Security Code which is composed of four-digit numeric characters.

  For iDevices (iPhone, iPod Touch, and iPad) running iOS 7 or later:  

  • Proceed to the Settings Menu and select iCloud.
  • Scroll down and look for Keychain. Toggle-on the option and confirm your Apple ID and your device passcode.
  • You’ll then be asked to input your mobile number.
  • Wait for the confirmation SMS from Apple.
  • Go back to the Settings Menu and look for the Safari pane.
  • Tap the AutoFill sub-setting. Toggle on the following features you want iCloud Keychain to use: credit cards, names, passwords,or addresses.

  Credit Card Management Facility    Apart from passwords, the Keychain can also store your credit card data on Apple’s server. So, the next time you visit an eCommerce site, you will no longer be required to re-enter your long credit card numbers, full name, billing address, and date & year of expiry.   If you feel that it’s a little bit odd to store this private information on Apple’s server, it’s practically the same concept as configuring your card details in your iTunes account. Here’s how you can set up your card details with iCloud:  

  • Go to the Safari Pane in the Settings Menu
  • Tick the AutoFill and Passwords option.
  • Tap on Saved Credit Cards
  • Select the Add Credit Card option. You’ll be asked to key in the Cardholder’s name, number, description, and expiry date.

  Limitation   This cloud-based password management service may be innovative, but it works within Apple’s limitations.It only operates with its native Safari browser. It doesn’t support Chrome and other third-party alternatives like Mozilla Firefox. But, unlike any other password manager, this one is totally free and cloud-based.   The introduction of this feature proves that cloud technology has a long way to go. It isn’t only limited to storing your digital files and applications. For something that comes for free, it’s a significant addition to the iOS and Mac OS X ecosystem.
