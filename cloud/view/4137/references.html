Extra content

Ed Vaizey, para-phrasing:

"I had the option to do a video talk, but preferred face-to-face.

E-accessibility is not something I knew much about as a shadow minister. I'll bring energy. 

We know pretty much all 16-24 year olds are online. That drops to 50% to over 65 year olds and drops further for over 75s.

Martha Lane Fox appointed by David Cameron as new Digital Champion. Remit to challenge government, public sector and industry.

Martha launched Manifesto for a networked nation, at Downing Street yesterday.

3 challenges from Martha:

1. [missed]

2. Close down web sites that consistently fail to address accessibility

3. Meet her detailed challenges - to be announced later this year. "

Already have an e-Access forum. Five terms of reference:

1. transpose the EU framework. Equivalent access.

Consultation document in September.

2. Existing forum's remit is to support business in exploiting e-accessibility. Listing businesses: Apple, Microsoft (support AbilityNet), BT, Google - who do something.

3. e-accessibility action plan. 5 work streams - 1) regulatory framework, look at consumer equipment (affordability),  4) remove barriers

Digital economy depends on everyone being enabled. Race4Life 2010 campaign.

We want a fair society."

Question from X, Department of Justice.

Question from Martyn Cooper - What constitutes "reasonable adjustments"? Related to the DDA/SENDA legistlation.

Question from Lima?, RNIB - TV on demand currently doesn't require captions/audio description. We have enviable standards for terrestial TV/broadcasters - and chance of changes? Ed, "yes, there is loophole, I suppose. And others have raised this issue. Legislation is a blunt instrument.

Question from X BBC, what about archiving sites that have been closed down?

Question from X RNIB, accessibility of mobile devices. Lot of discussion of Web, but are there any plans to support initiatives for mobiles? Ed citing Apple, and software again.

Question from X Dept of Work & Pensions. Hard to reconcile e-accessibility initiatives for (DWP) legacy systems with DWP 3.5 billion pound of cuts. Ed - always hard to reconcile aspirations with austerity.

Question from Johnathan Hassell, BBC. Picking up on Ed's point about new guidelines - suggest that we use existing standards, WCAG 2, British Standards etc. - session I'm chairing later on today. Also cost, basic web accessibility was not too expensive if plumbed in at start. Captions, audio descriptions though are expensive. Get BBC, Microsoft etc. on board.

Question from Howard Leicester, Research in Medical Informatics - are your statistics reliable? Is 50% correct? We have poor statistics on digital inclusion, those with disabilities, special requirements/needs. Ed's answer - not sure if I agree. Martha Lane Fox takes the view that the statistics are good enough. She's also said she can do a lot without much money. Ring fence OFCOM's budget for stastics/surveys.

Nick Freear
