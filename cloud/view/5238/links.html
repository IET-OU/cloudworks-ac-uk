How is creativity perceived in Denmark?

Cultural background

Creativity support in Danish HE

e-learning in Denmark

We wish that...

 

Denotation: richness in generating ideas, ability to apply ideas to practice

Connotations: creative hobbies (art, music etc.), creative disciplines, decadence, drug use etc., innovation in the sense of any new idea, genius

We tend to fail to grasp: i) the value of creative thought and theorizing, ii) creativity and creative potential in social contexts

 

Cultural traditionL EU change from classicism to romantic ideas: classicist - reason, the common good, facts and duty and romantic: emotions, the sovereignty of the individual, personal experiences. These traditions are strong in the entire Western world. The cold war may be perceived as a new classist time where reason and skills stood behind mankind and extinction and had to be proven on a daily basis. The time after the fall of the USSR may be perceived as a new romantic period, with the implications, that...

 

The romantic problem: we are confused and afraid. We cant see the place we want to go to. We hope to get in control of our situation. We hope to get in control of situation. Torn between valued freedom and control. 

 

Denmark has a strong romantic tradition, teacher as mentor. addition tradition for citizenship. learning to support for individual expression and achievement nourishing curiosity and supporting individual interests.  Also growing concern in decision making on how to cope with change and complexity which unfortunately leads to control orientated attempts to solve the educational challenges through: a) standardization (literary canons, competencies and measurable goals) and b) measuring (tests and benchmarking). While at the same time stressing the importance of creativity and innovation. 

 

E-learning (DK). Distance education, elearning used as support for flexible offers, lifelong learning. Learning objects and digitised resources used as a means of supporting more than one way to learn or a means of facilitating quick and effective learning of specific skills or competencies. Digitized texts assessments and benchmarking.  All this seems to reflect the romantic dichotomy between freedom or control. 

 

Wish list

  1. A new paradigm will emerge. Control and measurability point backwards. A new paradigm should promote and support qualified knowledge and skills rather than measurable achievement of predefined and specific goals. That we can help each other meet the challenge. According to Kuhn two different paradigms cannot communicate.
  2. A global population will learn from each other enhancing and sharing creative development 
  3. Virtual spaces and digitized content will become integrated in extremely flexible new forms of personally tailored educational paths supporting curiosity when possible laziness when necessary and individual and group achievements ad hoc. 
  4. Assessment forms will emerge that will embrace more or less any suitable and convincing presentation of qualified knowledge
