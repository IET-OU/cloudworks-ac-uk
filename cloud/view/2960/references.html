Discussing Articles 2.23.10 to 03.08.2010

  • Favourite
  • 734 views

Discuss several articles relevant to online teaching and learning with the authors

http://xli2010.ning.com/

Cloud created by:

Robert Squires
23 February 2010

Dear all,

As a prelude to our annual Montana University System Extended Learning Institute, we would like to invite you to participate in a discussion of several articles relevant to online teaching, learning and student support services. Several authors and collaborators will facilitate the discussions over the next two weeks--starting today! To join the discussion, simply ‘Sign Up’ on the Ning, select the article(s) you're interested in reading and reply to the discussion thread that has already started. Here is the link: http://xli2010.ning.com/

The articles and their facilitators are listed below:

(1)    Barczyk, C., Buckenmeyer, J.  & Feldman, L. (2010). Mentoring Professors:  A Model for Developing Quality Online Instructors and Courses in Higher Education. International Journal on E-Learning, 9(1), 7-26.

Facilitator: Mary Engstrom, Associate Director, Extended Learning Services, University of Montana.

(2)  Burnett, D.J. (2002). Innovation in Student Services: Best Practices and Process Innovation Models and Trends.  In D.J. Burnett & D.G. Oblinger, Innovation in Student Services: Planning for Models Blending High Touch/High Tech, (3-14). Retrieved January 14, 2010 from The University of Kansas Office for Student Success website: http://www.vpss.ku.edu/priorities/isis/documents/Best_Practices.pdf               

Facilitator: Candi Merrill, Manager of Learner Services, University of Montana.

(3)  Johnson, L., Levine, A., & Smith, R. (2010). The 2010 Horizon Report. Austin, Texas: The New Media Consortium. (Published at the end of January 2010)

Facilitator: Nick Noakes, Director of the Center for Enhanced Learning and Teaching in Hong Kong and Horizon Report panelist since 2005.

(4)  McNabb, L. & Olmstead, A. (2009). Communities of Integrity in Online Courses: Faculty Member Beliefs and Strategies.  MERLOT Journal of Online Learning and Teaching, 5(2), 208-221.  Retrieved January 11, 2010 from http://jolt.merlot.org/vol5no2/mcnabb_0609.htm

Facilitator: Alicia Olmstead, Assistant Integrity Officer, City of Austin, Texas.

Facilitator: Robert Squires, Instructional Designer, University of Montana.

(5)  Ludwig-Hardman, S. & Dunlap, J.C. (2003). Learner Support Services for Online Students: Scaffolding for success. The International Review of Research in Open and Distance Learning, 4(1), Article 4.1.4. Retrieved January 14, 2010 from http://www.irrodl.org/index.php/irrodl/issue/view/15

Facilitator: Joanne C. Dunlap, Faculty Fellow for Teaching & Associate Professor, University of Colorado.

Facilitator: Marlene Zentz, Instructional Designer, University of Montana.

(6)  Wherry, P. & Windes, D.L. (2010). When Distance Technologies Meet the Student Code. In Gearhart, D. (Ed.), Cases on Distance Delivery and Learning Outcomes: Emerging Trends and Programs (79-96). Hershey, PA: IGI Global.

Facilitator: Peg Wherry, Director of Online and Distance Learning at Montana State University
Facilitator: Robert Squires, Instructional Designer, University of Montana.

(7)  Loveland, K. A. (2007).  Student Evaluation of Teaching (SET) in Web-based Classes: Preliminary Findings and a Call for Further Research. The Journal of Educators Online, 4(2), Research Article.  Retrieved January 14, 2010 from http://www.thejeo.com/Archives/Volume4Number2/V4N2.htm#Volume_4,_Number_2,_July_2007

Facilitator:  Jason Neiffer, Curriculum Director, Montana Virtual Academy.

We are looking forward to sharing your thoughts and opinions.

Thank you,

Robert Squires

Instructional Designer, Extended Learning Services (XLS)

Continuing Education

The University of Montana - Missoula
