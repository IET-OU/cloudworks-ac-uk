Roger Mills - some thoughts with thanks to the UK Centre for Biosciences

 

Site has lots of examples of creativity in Biosciences, how relevant are these to developing creativity in ICT

How is creativity understood - do we mean teaching creatively or teaching creativity - they are intertwined

Can creativity be developed, does it need to start in the very early years?

Are creativity and imagination the same thing or can it destroy creativity or prevent it?

 

What can we learn from other disciplines? How are curiosity, confidence, environment, analogy, brainstorming, incubation and groups work - how are these used in ICT? 

 

Tips for promoting/teaching creativity: Creating the right environment: not just innovation labs, OXford biochem lab have a grand piano, speed dating sessions at p/g level to encourage students to come together from different disciplines, great advantage of collegiate universities where students meet across disciplines, be NOT afraid to fail. 

 

Mode 2 research need to be both interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary, working across disciplines and encourages across university and other potential stakeholders 

 

Encourage curiosity

Penicillin everyone know about this, Viagra - originally for high blood pressure, side effect male impotence, similarly Warfarin developed for destroying rats now used to treat heart problems

 

Challenging assumptions

Students need to question more

Cutting edge developments very susceptible to change

Student need to be made aware that not everything is already known

 

Use of analogy

Comparing 2 things which are different but have similarities

Identification of an analogy between a problem and a natural phenomenon - woodpecker and motor cycle helmets, Pringle crisp packages - wet and dry leaves

 

Scamper

Artifical blood (sibstitute)

Clockwork radio (combine clock and dynamo)

MIcrowave oven (adapt-radar technology)

Key hole surgery (modify)

Cupernicus (reverse earth orbits sun)

Pearnut (put to other uses - 300+)

 

Brainstorming/google storming

Post it sessions

Grid sessions more effective than traditional recording of thoughts on a flip chart

Organised random search/google storming: choose a page at random from a dictionary and use the words to help generate ideas, now one can use google or other search engines
