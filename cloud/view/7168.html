Dawn Schliesser: Reality-based eLearning

Tags:

Your situation (context)

 

I currently work in a corporate setting in which employees must be trained quickly and be able to perform job roles within a condensed period of time. Currently our eLearning is more didatic in nature and tends to simply be lecture with slides.

 

We have some great tools at our disposal including an AV studio, several nice development tools (complete toolkit), and a creative atmosphere within the learning and development team. Despite the money and effort placed in our learning and development branch, our employees struggle with a steep learning in which they are learning "bad habits" caused by necessity of working quickly and informal OTJ from tenured employees (employees who have their own way which is not necessarily how we need them to operate). 

Example

 

 

 

The change you would like to see (challenge)

 

What I want to do with the eLearning program I have been assigned is use a combination of story-based, real-life scenarios, and gamification to create a realistic immersive learning experience in which employees begin to learn how to apply the skills we need to teach them from day 1. This means less lecturing about systems and more about job tasks and how the systems support them. 

Example

 A new point of sale system is being launched and we will need to teach new and current employees how to use it. Scenario/story based learning will be employeed to highlight how one goes about doing their daily job activities in the new system. 

 

 

How you might go about bringing that change

 

I feel that a combination of cognitivisim (there will be a certain amount of "telling" that will need to be done based on skill level of the individual) and construcitvism needs to be employed in order to achieve the desired result, but at the moment I am leaning more towards constructivism in that I believe using problem-based learning or case-based learning will be a good place to start. The instructor can then use the eLearning based "cases" to coach and guide the learners towards applying the knowledge they need. I would also like to bring in elements from Thiagi and Ray Jiminez to shape some of the specific elements. 

 

I also want to leverage the culture of the company which is very geared toward networking and helping each other out. I believe this will fit nicely with a constructivist style of eLearning program. 

Example

 Discovery type eLearning course that allows learners to follow an outline of all the skills they need to master within a specific period of time and access to a coach that will meet with them x number of times a week to get ensure they are progressing adequately and a SME to answer any questions that come up.
