Designing a Welcoming Welcome session

  • Favourite
  • 475 views

Cloud created by:

Katherine Nicholson-Taylor
12 April 2016

Narrator

I facilitate the welcome session for our organisation.

 

Situation

We needed to design a welcome that wasn't sheep dipping staff through elements of training they didn't require therefore ensuring the best use of delegates time.  As an underlying theme, just as important as the Mandatory skills covered on our Induction session, we wanted our new starters to feel welcomed into our organisation and part of the overall team from the beginning.

 

Task

In order to measure our success in achieving our aim of attaining a welcoming role specific Induction we put together an evaluation form to be completed by delegates after their Induction process is finished to gather feedback on whether or not they found the Induction useful, welcoming and what they would change (if anything).   All completed forms are reviewed by the facilitators to aid us in measuring how successful the session is.

 

Actions

List the actions you took in chronological order. Note their effects – both expected and unexpected. Highlight any obstacles you encountered, and explain how you tried to overcome them.

 

We grouped together elements of training that all staff would benefit in attending and then elements that groups of staff would require for their roles allowing us to create a large group session first thing in the morning splitting off onto separate groups in the afternoon.  Some staff also return for a second day depending on their role.  This was easier said than done and some elements of training though essential to some staff groups were not included in Induction as we could only create a finite number of separate groups and options within the capacity of the number of rooms and trainers we had available.

 

Once established the logistics of room bookings and securing facilitators time as well as the admin resource to support the session with welcome packs' and useful paperwork was planned.

 

Welcome packs to aid learning by holding the detail important to Induction but not included int he face to face presentation had to be designed.

 

Results

The Induction received good feedback from staff regarding 'feeling welcome' and 'part of the team', we continue to alter the Induction on a regular basis based on the feedback forms we receive with small tweaks here and there and twice yearly we do an overhaul to ensure we are not letting content get stale or to lengthy which can occur as we add more and more smaller bits in based on feedback we have had.

 

Reflections

Upon reflection this experience, my first involvement in learning design, demonstrated how ever changing learning can be and how different groups, culture, genders etc. can respond to and experience the same element of learning/the same piece of information.

 

I appreciate the need to be flexible and willing to not only listen to feedback but act upon it.
