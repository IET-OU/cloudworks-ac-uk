Linda Wallinger - Virgnia Dept. of Education. Only three K-12 teachers in the room. Need to involve teachers. Now facing parents that have encouraged children to use online resoures - but not having it recognised in anyway. Need to link to end of course assessments - mandated by "no child left behind" - leads to high stake exams.

Legislation being passed to encourage open approaches to K-12. Board to review this (1 of whom is a K-12 educator). Also legislating for a virtual schools system. Hard for schools to judge quality of online provision from the many providers across the globe. Putting in place approval processes.

 

Comment 6 by Patrick McAndrew

JIm Fruchterman - Route66 literature trying to make a sustainable approach to helping teenager reading. And Bookshare providing shared scanning of books for those who have problems accessing content - e.g. text-to-speech. Raises issues of piracy - but legislation allows making accessible versions of copyright materials. Bookshare breakthrough was to win a $32m competition to give students access - now serving >90,000 students and hope to reach 200,000. Looking at making fulling accessible K-12 textbooks. Working on image accessibility. Working with Flatworld Knowledge to make them accessible. New R&D Centre with WGBH Boston to look at conversion.

Problem is that OER are not standardised - need to pay more attention to the publishers approach.

Comment 7 by Patrick McAndrew

Patrick McAndrew
2:59pm 9 April 2010 Permalink


DAve Zook - need to distill messages and develop a communication plan. Three key themes from consulting with stakeholders and legislators.

1. Important to focus on outcomes that OER provides

2. Be negative about the current system - know hte problem

3. Position OER as a solution - in concrete and discrete ways

Access is a particularly good argument to make - access to better outcomes matched to community needs. Message is "Learning beyond textbooks" that transforms. Orient discussion towards the future. Leading with K-12 but also addressing Higher Education. Will establish "Learning beyond textbooks" as a brand to promote.

Need - evidence! For messaging need better examples - experience within States and law. One or two critical examples may well be influential. Backed up with hard core evidence. Evidence driven advocacy needs supporting data!

Need to operate on the same page - with a coherent message.
