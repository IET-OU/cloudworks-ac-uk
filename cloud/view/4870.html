Understanding teachers' beliefs and the use of ICT

Beliefs are associated with practice, thus when a teacher holds a belief about a particular learning approach is likely to incorporate it in his/her classroom practice.

What happens when teacher's beliefs are in contradiction with governmental propositions?

What happens when available ICT tools are not aligned with teachers' beliefs about teaching and learning approaches?

Haven't thought about it actually. In case of evidence based practice, I feel that a continuous critical investigation of past and current practices while looking into obtained findings will of course have a constructive influence regarding future practices.

In the same perspective, I believe that a thorough investigation of teachers' belief systems regarding educational practice will enable educators to improve their understanding about teachers' beliefs and to what extend determine classroom practice. Consequently these findings will illuminate issues related to practice and beliefs and hopefully this evidence might improve teacher training and curriculum design.

I believe that teachers' set of beliefs have a control in a sense in every level of practice, either it is day to day teaching or training or even teaching preparation prior practice.

For example, in my country, Cyprus, the majority of science teachers (my interest is on science and physics as I am a Physics teacher) studied either in Cyprus or Greece. If we assume that in Greek and Cyprus Universities tend to follow a specific belief of what science is, then it is expected that this belief will be forwarded to secondary and primary schools despite governmental expectations. This is because the 'vehicle' of teaching and learning is the Teacher; therefore teachers consciously or unconsciously will follow their own educational beliefs, which are likely to be affected by their graduate studies, etc.

In the case of technology, the choice of tools and their use might be upon teachers' beliefs despite educational policies and curriculum designs; however there is always the problem of the equipment and the fear  of not achieving to use the tools in front of students.

I will  be glad to have your ideas and comments on this.
