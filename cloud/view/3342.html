I suggest we use the Nonaka and Takeuchi SECI model of the four modes of knowledge transfer to understand the AL's role.  Here are the modes:

1 - Socialisation (tacit individual knowledge to tacit individual knowledge)

2 - Externalisation (this is where the tacit concepts move towards becoming explicit and codified and get transformed into digital and paper format)

3 - Combination (this is the course material that is delivered to the student in the box)

4 - Internalisation (this is where the AL comes in.)

In course design at the OU BS the first three modes of knowledge transfer are the preserve of the course team that has written the material.  Course delivery is where the fourth mode of transfer comes in.

The tutor has to take the explicit material from the combination mode of knowledge transfer, add personal tacit knowledge to it--such as experience of business.  S/he helps the student to transform the explicit knowledge from the course team into tacit knowledge at a higher level.

Then the spiral takes another turn.  The student, through the externalisation mode of knowledge transfer will make explicit what s/he has learned, in the form of the tutor marked assignments).  At residential schools and in tutorials the "Socialisation" mode of knowledge transfer is used.

The four modes of knowledge transfer are explained in Nonaka & Takeuchi's book:

The Knowledge-Creating Company

Congratulations John on again pioneering a sharing technology for us. Quoting from your text about the tutor's role:

"S/he helps the student to transform the explicit knowledge from the course team into tacit knowledge at a higher level."

This is the key step from an AL viewpoint. What we could further explore is:

What is it that makes the difference between effective and ineffective  AL help for this transformation process? For most of the time we are in (remote) contact with students, it's not about knowledge transfer at all. The student is doing the transforming and the AL acts as a catalyst.

"Catalysts that speed the reaction are called positive catalysts. Catalysts that slow the reaction are called negative catalysts, or inhibitors." So we want to identify what makes us positive. Note also that  "Although catalysts are not consumed by the reaction itself, they may be inhibited, deactivated or destroyed by secondary processes"!

Thanks also from me to all colleagues for your insights and camaraderie. I'm hoping we will be able to make a lot of progress.

One quick point about the SECI model is that it has been heavily criticised by western KM academics for claiming that you can interconvert tacit and explicit knowledge. In practical terms, if you discard the tacit - explicit dimensions and concentrate on the 4 steps of the SECI cycle, it seems to provide a reasonable description of how teams create knowledge in practice. What is 'E'xternalised is perhaps better called information, i.e. knowledge minus the context and the knower. (A map doesn't read itself.)

So SECI is workable, but it's also right enough to be dangerously wrong, which perhaps offers a good teaching point about the need for critical thinking in the practical application of theory.

Hi Julian and all

 Determinism holds that everything is caused (determined) by a sequence of events that operate in a predictable way based upon past events .

This suggests that technological determinism is based on the idea that humans have little or no responsibility for controlling technology. The social constructivist model assumes the relevance of social groups who encourage flexibility in the design of the technology. The idea of whether virtual collaboration in VLE’s work effectively or not, as Weller (2006) suggests, depends on the level of contribution, and whether the approach aligns to the users’ own values.

Some argue that technology doesn't alter our behaviours but rather simply enables us to do the same things differently.

 Weller (2006) debates on how decentralization (i.e.no managerial control dictating how a technology is used) is key to e-learning processes. What does this say about the use of Virtual Learning Environment (VLE’s) ? Weller quotes Castell (2002),that' the internet can enable networks to compete against the traditional centralised models of control of learning and encouraging adaptability and flexibility.

From my experience in designing and moderating VLE’s via web ct for example-,the teacher has autonomy to decide on which tools are appropriate for the task at hand. So my feeling here Julian is that we should not be overwhelmed by the technology and be focused on the ‘must’ list of using tools post- first class usage, but more, that AL’s and students can decide on appropriate tools for achieving the task(s) at hand. It seems the OUBS are allowing us all in the AL community to trial /experiment without pre-determining which tools we should use in the new generation of VLE's. I think we need to re-assure the OUBS  AL community that this is an evolving process and the moodle VLE's in the first instance will be kept as simple as possible and that both learners and teachers are given e-space to develop e-learning innovations and in partnership betwen learner and provider. I gained a good slice of learning from students when triailing elluminate-but we were all in a 'level playing field'-learning together.

This may well suit the ‘digital natives of the future generations as suggested by Prensky (2001).

Whilst I agree with Fenella that pedagogy should drive the technology,however ,it is my personal view that practice-based management learning requires e-management development in this global network of e-communications in day to day e-business/e-commerce and social networking .I think we would all agree on this point.The OUBS e-learning should therefore also be seen as a 'process'  in management development.

Sometimes perhaps ,the 'big bang'  approach to introducing new technology is in the long run -more effective and research suggest that e-moderators should persist even when the going gets tough with e-hygiene factors ,Herzberg style:)

any thoughts  from the project teams ?
