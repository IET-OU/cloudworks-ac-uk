Comment 1 by Kevin Collins

Kevin Collins
7:25pm 16 March 2010 Permalink


I'm giving a short talk tomorrow on international perspectives on social learning.  A rather daunting task given the history of social learning, so I'll focus on my understanding of it in relation to natural resource managing. 

From my perspective, a key aspect of social learning is that it is more than just lots of people learning in a particular space or context.  It has a sense of purpose (often learned in the process) and those participating in a social learning process have a sense in which they are learning for concerted action.  This focus on concerted action may be more relevant to environmental situations than others - perhaps something to discuss. 

I also understand social learning as more than participation -something that I hope we can discuss tomorrow.

Talk covered:

Brief history of socaillearning - Tarde (1850)/Rotter(1950)/Bandura(1977).

Examples from Environmental Science of SL: Projects on water management - UN IHDP. Social Learning Group (2001) gathered together background research.

Definitions - different points of views, boundaries, rules and processes to go beyond an individual capability. Apply to solve complex (environmental) situations. HIerarchy of information -> consultation -> participation -> social learning.

Characterised by cjhanges in practice as well as in understanding. Matches to system view of working together for a "performance". Can such a learning system be designed?

  • Purposeful
  • not deterministic
  • knowledge may be contested

A soft system view then allows mapping of the system or technology to support the processes of sociallearning.
