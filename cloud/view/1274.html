Dan Sutch: Engaging with technological and social changes in education

  • Favourite
  • 1087 views

Presentation at CAL 09 conference Brighton

Cloud created by:

Gráinne Conole
23 March 2009

Part of Keri Facer's symposium at CAL 09 on the Beyond current horizon's project.

Looking at public and stakeholder engagement – large list from learners, teachers, parents, policy makers, employers, etc
Importance of agency – multiple actors – ability to act in and on the world within multiple possible futures

Wider perspectives and insight – informed participation to increase insight and perspectives within VBCH

Futurelab approach – voice, participation, co-design/co-development


Growing engagement agenda – civic participation, active citizenship etc

Three principles
1.    Deliberative engagement
2.    Normative/typical engagement
3.    Communications/dissemination

Approaches used

  1. Millionfutures.org.uk , 1539 entries from 100 different counties
  2. Series of questions about the future, including what are your hopes for the future, what do you want your community to be like, what are your biggest worries – etcPowerleague.org.uk How to educate league, what education for league
  3. Citizen panel – 10 questions sent to 1100 members of the public, 513 respondents
  4. Citizen council – senior reps from organisation set up to work for particular groups within society 1. Current values of education, 2. Prioritising emerging evidence from the 5 challenges, 3. Future aims of education.
  5. Stakeholder workshop and interviews with a wide range of participants

Thinking critically about the future
1.    Questioning assumptions – expectation that things will remain/change
2.    Qualifying conversations of the future with examples from the present
3.    Frames of reference

Futures thinking provides an opportunity to be critical of current educational policy/practice
Digital technology in education is still very contested – range of responses, from vital for future of education to concerns of technology overload and dehumanisation of education, emerging technologies were not mentioned at all
A lot of the questions were about the values of education
