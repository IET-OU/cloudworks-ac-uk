Title Cath Wilson- Design narrative for Medicines Administration workshop

 

Narrator

My role is a nurse educator working in post registration professional development. I aim to refresh the importance of medicines administration safety.

 

Situation

The workshop is designed in a service situated faculty of education within an acute hospital setting. The candidates attend the session facilitated by myself , in work time, at the recommendation of their manager. There are no academic credits attached to the workshop however completion of online pre-work (Moodle) is essential admission criteria. The workshop is not mandatory however recommended as a tool to assist return to normal duties following a medication administration error.

 

Task

Increased Safety awareness of medication administration is the aim of the session to assist the practitioner. The candidates have to achieve 100% on the online MCQ prior to attending the face to face session to demonstrate knowledge, in class they will debate clinical scenarios with support of other candidates and their engagement in class should be noted. The intention is to restore confidence to the practitioner to return to drug administration role and we would expect a reminder of the processes to follow would prevent another incident. In terms of public and patient assurance it is a part of staff development.

 

Actions

(Incident occurs, referred to workshop Lead)

Enrolled to Moodle to access resources

Undertake MCQ, gain 100%-multiple attempts allowed

Attend f2f workshop

Discuss errors

Debate clinical scenarios in groups

Didactic sessions on diabetes & electronic prescribing

Viewing patient story video

End of course Moodlework

 

Results

Expected outcomes is candidate completion of course and return to duties.

Addititonal outcomes- the groups highlighted inequity in approach to managing a drug error, therefore a Strategic framework has been put in place.

 Patient videos were the most powerful resource, evidenced through evaluations and recall.

 

Reflections

The key learning points as the facilitator was;

The session has value to individual due to the pastoral support.

Not to rely on staff sharing experiences as some have been handled very differently.

Patient story videos are powerful but have the potential to derail a session and incite fear rather than caution.
