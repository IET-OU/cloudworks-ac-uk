Listing of Project Exhibition Stands at the event

Cloud created by:

JIF2010
12 July 2010

  • Stand #1 - NeISS - Providing an infrastructure for social simulation - University of Manchester

NeISS is providing a platform to support powerful social simulation scenarios.  This includes tools for composing simulation, visualisation, crowd sourcing and sharing of research.   

http://www.neiss.org.uk

  • Stand #2 - Working within the community for the Manchester Data Management Pilot - University of Manchester

MaDAM is working towards a pilot infrastructure as input towards a potential university-wide data management service for the University of Manchester.

http://www.library.manchester.ac.uk/aboutus/projects/madam/

  • Stand #3 - Enable - Staffordshire University

Enable is focused on addressing the issues around the underlying data, roles and applications that support processes around Curriculum Design and Development.

http://projects.staffs.ac.uk/enable

  • Stand #4 - Making assessment count through e-reflection - University of Westminster

This project will describe the process that students and staff go through to enable better use to be made of the feedback that students receive on their coursework.

https://sites.google.com/a/staff.westminster.ac.uk/mac/

  • Stand #5 - Developing a blended repository of OA research and UKOER at Leeds Metropolitan University using the intraLibrary repository platform - Leeds Metropolitan University

This project is exploring the challenges of using a common software platform for OA research/OER.

http://repository.leedsmet.ac.uk/

  • Stand #6 - Application of Service Design Mapping for HE business processes - University of Derby

This project applied service design and enhancement techniques to the student lifecycle: from application to enrolment and progression. 

www.derby.ac.uk/experience/jisc-enrolment-project

  • Stand #7 - 5 curriculum representations: from macro to micro - Open University, Institute of Educational technology 

This exhibition will introduce the 5-view framework being used by the project to represent the curriculum.

Go to the OULDI project's 5- view framework poster

  • Stand #8 - Languages Open Resources Online (LORO) - Open University

LORO is based at the OU and aimed at languages professionals. We will show how LORO works, what the philosophy behind the project is and how it interacts with other repositories.

http://www.open.ac.uk/education-and-languages/loro/

  • Stand #9 - GrassPortal: ecological and evoluionary data for all the world’s grass species - University of Sheffield

GrassPortal will be a “one-stop-shop” for researchers and educators. This exhibition will explain how the portal will integrate the latest distribution, evolutionary history and ecological data.

www.grassportal.org

  • Stand #10 - JISC Supporting, Harnessing and Advancing Repository Enhancement (SHARE) - Nottingham Trent University

This presentation will show audio interviews of staff illustrating benefits of resource sharing.

http://www.ntushare.org/

  • Stand #11 - JISC Organisation and User Technologies (OUT) Team stand
  • Stand #12 - UK Institutional Repository Search, Names Project and IESR (electronic resource catalogue) - Mimas, University of Manchester & Mimas, British Library

The UK Institutional Repository Search (IRS) Project currently searches over 102 UK HE institutional repositories, accessing around 500,000 deposited documents and theses.

www.intute.ac.uk/irs/demonstrator

  • Stand #13 - Poetiks: An E-Learning App to Teach Poetic Technique - Bath Spa University

Poetiks is designed to help students learn to understand and appreciate the significance of poetic techniques.

  • Stand #14 - JISC E-Learning Team stand
  • Stand #15 - APEL – the Pineapple Project - University of Plymouth 

The Pineapple Project is developing a web-based tool to guide HE in FE staff through APEL processes and provide appropriate support.

www.pineappleproject.org.uk

  • Stand #16 - Automated citation feeds from institutional repository (CentAUR) to the web CMS - University of Reading

Automated citation lists from CentAUR populate web CMS pages, replacing diverse, labour intensive school run processes.

http://centaur.reading.ac.uk

  • Stand #17 - Visualising China Project - ILRT, University of Bristol

This project will demonstrate software for enhancing the online search/browse of Bristol’s collection of 7000 digitised historical photographs of China. 

http://visualisingchina.ilrt.bris.ac.uk

  • Stand #18 - JISC Advance

Focused on the needs of users JISC Advance brings together the collective expertise of established JISC services: JISC Digital Media, JISC infoNet, JISC Legal, JISCMail, JISC Netskills, JISC Procureweb, JISC RSC UK and JISC TechDis. The resources which JISC Advance provides address key challenges within higher education, further education and the skills sector: Efficiency and effectiveness; Enhancing learning and teaching; External engagement; Research; and Sustainability.

http://www.jiscadvance.ac.uk/
