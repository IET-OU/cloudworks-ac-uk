Keynote by Josie Taylor at CAL 09 March 09

Cloud created by:

Gráinne Conole
31 March 2009

Outlined some of her research background

  • Original worked with Ben de Boulay
    O’Shea, Scanlon and O’Malley work on mediated eye contact and its role in establishing collaboration in solving problems of running in the rain, successful collaboration being evidente in discourse patterns (eg finishing each others sentences)
  • Mobile learning 0 Sharples, Vavoula, McAndrews conversational processes of becoming informed about each others practice

Used the analogy of the emergence of the paperback bok as an example of technology innovation

  • Paperback book as a technology
  • Changed size, access, costs what didn’t device functionality, skills caused a revolution in public reading habit
  • Ubiquity – everyone has a connection
  • Ambience – everyeone carries a connection
  • Flow
  • Connection
  • Semantic web, The grid – massive amount of power, ubiquity – devices permantely available,
  • Implications – the compliant learner is long dead , but what if the client wants something that doesn’t support learning?, what if the context of learning is disturbed transient or fleeting?

Learning context of the future

  • Increased network coverage, non—compliant learners, networks that understand data, that can reconfigure and repair themselves, return of the intelligent machine???
  • Are we good at guiding teachnology development for learning?
  • Computers as participants in a more cybernetic perspective –what would a framework for telling a computer what to do would look like

A theory of mobile learning

  • The process of coming to know across diff contexts
  • Conversation is the driving process of learning  it is the means by which we negotiate differences, understand each others experiences and form transiently stable interpretations

Conversation and context

  • Pask’s 1976 conversation theory, Laurillard, 2002
  • Vygitskian views of intrasubjectivity and intersubjectivity
  • Engestrom’s extended activity theory frameowkr
  • Subject – learner, obkect – knowledge ad sils
  • Contolr –social rules, context – community, communication  conversation and division of labour, signs – learningresources
  • Take two different views of the same situation – from the learner perspective (semiotic layer) and the technological perspective (tool view)
  • Continual dynamic between the semiotic and technological
  • Dialectical process of appropriation can be mapped – Waycott 2004
  • Creation of meaning is in the exchange between participants in the conversation
  • Continual co-evolution of tools and users

Grand challenge for computing research in learning for life
We need more comprehensive theories of learning in the digital world

We need interdisciplinary effort
Need to understand more how technologies and users co-evolve
A society which is mobile which is full of channels for the distribution of a change occurring anywhere, must see to it that its members are educated to personal initiative and adaptability. Otherwise, they will be overwhelmed by the changes in which they are caught and whose significance or connections they do not perceive.
(Dewey 1916: 88)
