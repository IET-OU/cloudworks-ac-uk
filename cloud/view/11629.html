Comparing 4T's Model and DPD

  • Favourite
  • 147 views

Cloud created by:

Annette Hendley
28 March 2018

The 4T’s design seems task and time focused. The teams and technology will depend on the task that needs to be completed.

It reminds me of a typical lesson design for the classroom. On the other hand, if time is flexible, the time feature can be ignored without influencing the design. The visual representation is useful to give an overview of the structure and to clarify the design.

Without including the time component, I would say my design follows this structure. My design allows the task to form a hierarchy from reading the text to worksheets, which aids, but also requires individual work. The student needs to make a choice and then complete an individual task without assistance. As it is 1:1 tutoring discussions will only be between the tutor and the student.

DPD is less time bound and more fluid, but it also allows for choices to be made. While the generic design doesn’t propose a hierarchy the lesson design had certain steps. Even though there is no visual representation for the specific lesson the generic representation of DPD is clear and shows the meta principal which looks at the bigger picture and explains the concept of ‘showing thinking’ while the pragmatic principle is more focused on visual aids around the inquiry process and then split into specific inquiry choices. The phenomena researched with dynamic visuals. The idea of reusing the students’ own artefacts resonates and reminded me of the What If video and Book Creator that allow student artefacts to become more visible and have utility.

While my activity is text based and I attempted a visual representation of all the different components the student needs to remember to be able to answer a specific question, more visual aids will be beneficial.

The main difference though seems to be the overarching meta principle for DPD where the 4T’s Model doesn’t state that explicitly.

I am adding this because I want to remember it.

 Meta principle allows for building on student ideas, communicate the rich diversity of inquiry, flow of activities, cognitive conflict, complex projects, reasoning templates, support for adaption, reduce visual complexity, reuse artefacts and connect to personal context.
