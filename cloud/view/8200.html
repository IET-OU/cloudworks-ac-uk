19th March 11.40 - 12.30: Engaging with the research community - Peer led interactive session

  • Favourite
  • 548 views

Led by Pooja Sinha (ARTS), Mair Lloyd (FELS), Clare Mumford (OUBS), Catherine Seale (MCT), Vickie Curtis (MYB 1-4)

Cloud created by:

Vrecka Scott
6 March 2013

Aims and Objectives:

With the one hour session on each day this year, we want to touch on networking, reflecting on progress and engaging with the research community, with an emphasis on student interaction. These are guiding themes which we will use to open up the discussion while inviting student participation - rather than being strictly topic-led, we envisage this session as giving attendees the opportunity to ask us any question they may have regarding the PhD process.  

What will be covered?:

Engaging with the research community

-          engaging creatively with the research topic

-          gain a fuller understanding of the wider research environment

-          being proactive about presenting at conferences

-          being able to communicate one’s research to a non-specialist audience of academics and/or interested professionals

 The onus is on the researcher to translate their work into the wider research context. Such a project can be taken up at any point during the PhD, and in small ways. Two examples will be discussed at length -  

 1. The OU Poster Competition:

This is a good way to present one’s research topic to a non-specialist audience; most students like to do it in their first year. We will showcase last year’s posters from across discipline where students can talk to presenters, if they are available and think about possible ways to present their own research.

 2. Work in Progress Seminars

A student friendly platform that welcomes discussions about on-going projects and where an interested peer group functions as a valuable sounding board for developing research ideas. WIPS offers feedback and encouragement, and students often ask their friends from different disciplines to come and hear about their work. It prepares students to engage a non-specialist audience, which further implies unpacking jargon and aiming for a clear and coherent communication of ideas. After each presentation, students can take the opportunity to reflect on what makes for a successful presentation and how to engage audiences. Material prepared for the WIPS, together with the feedback offered, can be further fine-tuned for presentations at bigger conferences and/or academic papers.

 Beyond the Session: Carrying the discussion forward

 We suggest that the discussions generated during these two interactive sessions can be taken forward on another platform which enables continuing participation.  On SocialLearn, students can lead discussions and focus on areas that interest them most.

SocialLearn provides students the opportunity to create their own learning paths as well as explore the discussion paths that have been created – there are numerous paths that provide information on good practice, links to training skills and so on. The significance of such a platform is in terms of the opportunity to network across disciplines, create new communities of mutual interest with user-generated content, and to combine learner support with online resources. The concerns raised during the sessions can be further answered by the wider research community on the virtual platform, thus carrying the discussion forward across the boundaries of academic specialisation and geographic location.
