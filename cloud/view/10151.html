10th birthday event: the last 10 years and a look into the future - 22 November 2016

  • Favourite
  • 402 views

Cloud created by:

Graham Healing
2 November 2016

The eLearning Community is 10 this year so to celebrate we held a special birthday event featuring four presentations reviewing the changes that have taken place over the last 10 years as they have affected four topics that we have featured regularly since the earliest days of the eLC.

 Where: Library Seminar Rooms 1 & 2

 When: 10:00 -12:30, Tuesday 22 November 2016

 Recording: Available to OU staff on Stadium.

The presentations

  • 10:00     Introduction from Chris Pegler (founder of the eLC)
  • 10:15     Back to the Future of Mobile Learning: Mike Sharples (IET)
  • 10:45     Citizen science and biodiversity recording at the OU - from Evoluation MegaLab to iSpotnature and beyond: Janice Ansine and Mike Dodd (STEM)

11:15     Break for tea, coffee and cake

  • 11:30     10 years of OpenLearn: a little bit of history: Patrina Law (OMU)
  • 12:00     Learning languages online: Tita Beaven (WELS)
  • 12:30     More cake and conversation

 

Extracts

Back to the Future of Mobile Learning: Mike Sharples - Institute of Educational Technology

In 2000 I gave a presentation that attempted to map out, with illustrations, the next 15 years of mobile learning. I will use that presentation as a basis to discuss which predictions have been realised, which are still to be achieved and why, and what are possible futures for mobile learning.

10 years of OpenLearn: a little bit of history: Patrina Law - Free Learning, Open Media Unit

In October 2016, OpenLearn became 10 years old. This presentation will be a short review of the site, its origins, growth and impact and plans for the future. We’ll be looking at some of the biggest hitting interactives and editorial pieces, along with how our approach to making OpenLearn courses has been influenced since the advent of digital badging and MOOCs.

Pedagogy, technology, research: reflecting on 10 years of innovation in languages at the OU: Tita Beaven - School of Languages and Applied Linguistics, WELS

In this talk, I will look back at the projects that colleagues from the Department of Languages (now School of Languages and Applied Linguistics) have presented at eLC events over the past 10 years to highlight the key areas of our work on open education, evaluation of new technologies, and assessment. I will emphasize the  synergies between teaching and research, which I believe are at the core of enhancing the quality of teaching and learning. 

Citizen science and biodiversity recording at the OU - from Evolution MegaLab to iSpotnature and beyond: Janice Ansine and Mike Dodd - School of Environment, Earth and Ecosystem Sciences, STEM

Over the past 10 years The Open University (OU) has successfully extended teaching and learning about biodiversity beyond the parameters of the laboratory or lecture hall through citizen science; expanding possibilities for public participation in biological recording through innovative educational technology. From www.evolutionmegalab.org, which marked the centenary of Charles Darwin’s birth; to www.iSpotnature.org the OU’s flagship citizen science initiative; and www.Treezilla.org aimed at cataloguing Britain’s trees and recording the value of their ecosystem services, providing a framework for understanding why trees are important in an accessible way which anyone can understand.

These initiatives are now also part of the OpenScience Lab www.opensciencelab.ac.uk, where learners can take their interest in nature and practical science further. This presentation shares experiences from these initiatives, particularly the success of iSpot; which engages people as citizen scientists, encouraging learning about wildlife while building species identification skills.
