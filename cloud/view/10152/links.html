Direct authoring - 13 December 2016

  • Favourite

The eLearning Community will be discussing direct authoring of learning materials. There will be presentations from teams that have been experimenting with direct authoring in the OpenEdX platform as part of the Minerva project and from the OpenCreate project, the OU's initiative to integrate direct authoring into the current VLE.

 

Where: Library Seminar Rooms 1 & 2

 When: 10:00 -12:30, Tuesday 13 December 2016

 

  • 10:00   Why Minerva wanted to trial direct authoring: Lucy Gray, Minerva champion (WELS)
  • 10:15   Experiences from Minerva project colleagues:
    • 10:15   S309 Earth processes: Angela L Coe (STEM)
    • 10:35   F79 MA in Translation: Severine Hubscher-Davidson, Tita Beaven and Anna Comas-Quinn (WELS)
    • 10:55   Questions and discussion
  •  11:15   Tea and coffee
  •  11:30   OpenCreate: Background and overview: Andrew McDermott (LTS)

Abstracts

Why Minerva wanted to try direct authoring

Lucy Gray, Minerva Champion

Minerva is a 2 year strategically funded project which has  the aim of inspiring and supporting faculties to improve models of curriculum design and module development, based on agile methods, innovative pedagogies and new educational technologies. The project has been exploring a range of technologies and pedagogies but a core strand has been developing technologies and ways of working to enable direct authoring. Direct authoring provides an exciting challenges to the platforms and technology used, the roles of everyone contributing to module development and the processes involved.  

 

S309 Earth Processes

Angela L. Coe (STEM)

S309 is the pioneering new mainstream Open University module that has been created by academics directly authoring into a learning management system (Open edX). Authors have found this an empowering, efficient and engaging process. The team have also (i) changed the communication style with students; (ii) designed new pedagogical packages and approaches; (iii) fully co-operated; and (iv) as a core part of the middle months of production ran five pilots with students to test and develop ideas. The early success of the approach taken by S309 was a key part of the inspiration for both Project Minerva and OpenCreate. The seminar will present results and feedback from the pilots and first 2 months of the 2016J presentation.

 

F79 MA in Translation

Severine Hubscher-Davidson, Tita Beaven and Anna Comas-Quinn (WELS)

The MA in Translation is a postgraduate qualification consisting of three 60-credit modules, which will present in February 2017, October 2017 and June 2018. Production started in May 2016 and the modules are being directly authored into the OpenEdX platform. In order to deliver this rapid production and to overcome the complexity of delivering material for five language combinations, the team chose to use direct authoring on the OpenEdX platform. This largely freed us to experiment with alternative production models without being tied to existing systems and processes. The contribution will go over the advantages of working in such a way and the challenges that the team has encountered along the way.
