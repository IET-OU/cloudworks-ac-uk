Delivering Learning Programmes on FutureLearn

Cloud created by:

Julie Norton
16 March 2017

This event will look at the OU’s recent initiatives of offering suites of MOOCs through FutureLearn and will include presentations from the Languages and Business areas

 Where:          Library Seminar Rooms 1 & 2

 When:           14.00 – 16.00, Wednesday 29th March 2017 

 Recording:    Available to OU staff on Stadium

  • 14:00 - 14:45         Is the pain worth the gain? (Hillary Collins, Business School) 

    Hilary Collins, Lecturer and Student Experience Manager at The Open University Business School

    What role can Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) play in enhancing the perceived value of higher education? Whether or not MOOCs will revolutionize higher education or whether they are a passing fad is perhaps of less importance. They can perhaps be seen as creating value – from the learners’ perspective- but is the pain worth the gain?

  • 14:45 - 15:00         Coffee break 

  • 15:00 - 15:45         Designing and delivering a language MOOC on FutureLearn (Zsuzsanna Bárkányi and Anna Proudfoot, School of Languages and Applied Linguistics) 

    Language MOOCs have now been added to the ever-growing list of open courses offered by various providers, including FutureLearn. The School of Languages and Applied Linguistics at The Open University launched its first language MOOCs, a programme of six Spanish for Beginners courses from August 2016, and a programme of six Italian for Beginners courses from September 2016, all delivered on the FutureLearn platform. They contained activities designed to enhance receptive (reading, listening) and productive (writing, speaking) language skills, and encouraged learners to use the target language from the very beginning.

    In a massive course, where a discussion can attract hundreds of comments, moderation, support and feedback represent a big challenge for the lead educators and moderators, and the heterogeneous composition of the learners, who come from a range of countries and backgrounds, also requires a certain level of intercultural awareness both in learners and educators.

    We will offer an insight on the experiences of designing and delivering language MOOCs in Italian and Spanish on FutureLearn, and will discuss the opportunities and challenges encountered, and what worked and what didn’t, paying special attention to the challenges posed by teaching (and learning) speaking skills with no face-to-face or synchronous contact.

The event takes place during Open Education Week and will be live streamed to an international audience. The recording of the event will also be made available openly outside the university on Stadium.
