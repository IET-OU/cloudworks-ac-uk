Live Blog of talk: Michael Hannafin, University of Georgia

Learning without instruction? Technology and the emergence of student-centered learning environments

Long journey down a very winding road

  • Strict behavioural psychologist
  • Then came my own children
  • Interests evolved to cognitive – then to learning no longer defined by external goals and structures
  • Learning not limited to children – evident in myriad resources on the Web in homes, work, schools and everyday life

Ideas against student-centred learning (for example Sweller, Clark, etc.)

  • Cognitive load
  • Student control/management
  • Metacognitive demands
  • Orientation-disorientation
  • Efficiency
  • Others(s)?

Argument for student-centered learning

  • How do people learn?
  • Goals, assumptions, methods vary dramatically
  • Research and theory also vary dramatically
  • Pervasive in formal and informal settings
  • So much out there!
  • Need to understand both potentials and perils

Some quotes

De Jong – the great challenge will be to find load reducing methods…. Ways of learning from multiple representations, game-based learning, inquiry-based learning. I.e. we need to think of these as a different class of learning activities

Susan Land’s work… how can you learn something when you have very little based level understanding of the area, how do you do it, where do you start

Today’s focus

How, when and why do we learn without instruction?

Review similarities and distinctions: research theory and practice for directed v student centered teaching and learning

Describe how resources and distributed space enhance

Revisiting learning, knowledge and skill

Working constructs:

  • Different learning types, formal, rote, informal, free-choice, vicarious, intended , incidental, etc
  • Different knowledge types – verbal info, hierarchical (intellectual), psychomotor, generative, tool, situated, problem solving, etc.
  • Different skills types – cognitive, psychomotor, affective, problem solving, reasoning (deductive, inductive), inquiry, etc.

Does the same approach work equally well for all types of learning, knowledge and skill? Richard Mayer says yes, Richard Clark they argue we need to direct student activity

If, not, what approaches should we use?

Learning without instruction

The evolution of teaching and learning

  • Influences within/beyond social sciences
  • Learning, knowledge, skill in sciences, mathematics, etc.?
  • Decentralization of learning opportunities
  • Pervasive roles of technology – home, schools, workplace; dramatic increases in available ‘assets’; social networking – formal and informal
  • The quest for better/different models

Student-centered learning

What is it?

  • Learning goals – who establishes? Assesses? Determines when goals have been met?
  • Learning method/approach – who determines what will be available/required? Which approaches appropriate/necessary? When to use which?
  • How can the practical goals of educational and training addressed?

Interplay between goals and means: a working model: 4 quadrants; 2 dimensions

  • External learning goals vs. student learning goals
  • Externally provided structures vs. student-generated structures

Resource-based learning environments – LPSL conceptual and research activities

Heuristics

  • Resources
  • Enabling context(s)
  • Tools
  • Scaffolds

Resource types and functions: static/fixed – textbook, film, video, static websites; dynamic – human (teacher, experts, peers, family members, etc.); technology-based (databases, website, social networks, wikis, chat rooms, etc.)

Context types and functions: externally imposed – class assignment, course projects, work tasks/requirements, etc.; Learning-generated: in-the-moment, curiosity-driven, self-assigned; Negotiated: either goals or means set externally, learner determines either how to pursue OR what they what to gain/learn

Tool types and functions: searching/seeking (Google, BING, etc.), manipulating (spreadsheets, Second Life, simulations), processing (concept mapping, brainstorming (e.g. Inspiration, CSILE environment), communicating (discussion boards, chats, teleconference, tweeting)

Scaffold types and functions: procedural (help, on-demand guidance, site maps, etc), metacognitive (reminders to relate to PK, self-checking gauging ongoing understanding), conceptual assist the learner to decide what is important, what to prioritise, can be learner-generated

Problems, issues and implications

  • Will students seek and compare different perspective or learn only a particular perspective?
  • Will students analyze and interpret meaning critically?
  • Will students inquire critically?
  • Will students discriminate sources credibility?
  • Will students locate, assemble meaning, and construct understanding?

The future of student-centered learning?

  • Do we respond, support and optimise student-centered learning?
  • Will we be able to scaffold learning where the unique goal of individuals are not now a priori?
  • Will formal education be tied more to the past (and problem) than the future (and solution)?
  • We can control curriculum and to some extent how learning is supported, but not what/how individuals choose to learn
