Why blended?

Can be traced back to the 1920…s called supervised correspondence study (Moore, 2006, The handbook of blended learning)

It’s what we do already, very well…

A defining approach

Blended learning at Massey University is about purposely integrating different resources, environments and delivery modes to provide an exceptional and distinctive experience for all students

Combines face-to-face, class, and home or work place

Combines campuses

Combines modes

Combines staff and disciplines

Combines learning resources

NOT just about face-to-face and online!

Australian survey of students (CEQ) survey

Course Experience Questionnaire – every student gets this from the Government, response rate is ca. 70%, to gather views on their experience

‘To identify those components of their university experience that students identify as most engaging them in productive learning’

14 universities, 94, 835 comments

Ranking of importance

Course Design 40.6%

Staff: 24.4%

Support: 10.7%

Outcomes: knowledge/skills 3.8%

It’s the total experience that’s important and that is what shapes students judgements. Want a sound, responsive relevant clear and mutually reinforcing course design, using an appropriate mix of interactive, practice-orientated and problem-based learning methods.

Also want to see improvements in the design during implementation

Efficient and responsive admin, IT, library and student support systems actively working together

Blended learning at Massey

Received a significant number of responses demonstrating that staff are doing at Massey

Visual and oral instruction for distance students, mixing online, block and face to face, using mobile phone technologies, theoretical-applied, international student collaboration online, using field diagnosis, scenarios, small groups and large groups, virtual hospitals, class room emulations online, filmed lectures live and recorded, CDs, revisions CDs, online interactions, problem-based, internships, simulations

Planning to develop an exemplar database
