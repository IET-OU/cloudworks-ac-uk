Practical Teaching Sessions for my JEB/EDI Teaching Diploma

 

Narrator

I was the teacher who wrote the statement of work, course outline, learning objectives and all course materials for a “hands-on” session on how to use the Google Apps (Google Mail and Google Calendar)

 

Situation

 

8 of my friends attended a real-life training session in the computer training room of a local Dublin college in March 2011.

 

As well as the attendees, I was present as the teacher and an assessor was also present taking notes and observations

 

The attendees were there voluntarily to provide assistance to myself as the teacher who was being evaluated on the basis of this session.  They also were attending in order to learn something/gain knowledge from the session

 

As the teacher being assessed I had to provide the best possible experience to the students in order to pass my evaluation.

 

My assessor was there in order to see whether I had gained enough knowledge and skill from my evening diploma course in order to successfully run a hands-on IT training course.

 

Describe the physical, social and intentional factors that define the design space, e.g. where and when did this occur? In what kind of space? Who were the key actors, and what were the relations between them? What were the beliefs and desires that shaped their interaction?

 

Task

 

The course consisted of a number of elements

(i) Summary of key concepts and background by myself as the teacher

(ii) Show and tell practical demonstrations

(iii) Worked examples

(iv) Review with questions

 

The measure of success was the number of students who completed the work examples successfully (98%) and also the feedback given on the course evaluation form (95% positive)

 

 

Actions

List the actions you took in chronological order. Note their effects – both expected and unexpected. Highlight any obstacles you encountered, and explain how you tried to overcome them.

 

Results

 

Most of the students completed the worked examples will little or no assistance

 

Feedback questionnaires were positive with all students saying they learned something from the session.

 

Some of the worked examples did not work out as planned, so I had to change one of them on the whiteboard, in real time.

 

Students responded that the written material was of excellent quality and all were eager to take it with them after the session.

 

Feedback questionnaire results did state that the room was too warm and it highlights the importance of environmental issues in these situations.

 

 

Reflections

 

  • You can never have enough preparation, double check and double check.
  • All worked examples that are dependent on certain responses in computer software need to be fully tested
  • Written materials need to be100% up to date as students will pick up differences very quickly
  • Pause, slow down and take a breath when explaining concepts to students
  • Always test understanding
  • Check that you can adjust the room temperature eg. By opening a window or increasing central heating.
