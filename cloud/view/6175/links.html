Preparing for Probation Review (2012)

  • Favourite
  • 976 views

Cloud created by:

Rebecca Ferguson
9 March 2012

Led by Dr Regine Hampel.

This session gave an introduction to the probation assessment which PhD students need to pass to move into the second stage of their doctoral studies. Passing the probation assessment concludes the first year of registration for full-time students. For part-time students it concludes the first two years. The probation assessment consists of 4 elements (probation report, mini-viva, skills audit and oral presentation) and the presentation described each of these in detail. Variations by faculty were also discussed.

Timelines for probation assessment

Generally, the full-time PhD students will do it at the end of the first year while for the part-time students, it comes at the end of  the second year. But the part-time students can do it earlier. The timeline is flexiable and the students should discuss this with their supervisors.
