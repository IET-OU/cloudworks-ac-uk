Play Spaces By Angela Bonehill

  • Favourite
  • 652 views

Cloud created by:

angela bonehill
25 March 2016

Summary

This narrative shows the design of a scenario for students to design a ‘play space’ to encourage learning for children in early years

Context

Age: 18+ Level 4 (First year of University) Early Childhood students at University. Lecturer was interested in how students would plan, work collaboratively and design a play space for children.

Material

Students are presented with a bag of model making resources, empty boxes, tubes and bottles, tape, string and glue. Each group has a separate room to work together for one hour. A 7 year old child will assess the learning possibilities and the durability of the play space.

Situation

Students are in their 3rd module and have had lessons on child development, communication and collaboration. We have also learnt about the importance of play in relation to children’s learning.

The students work well in their preferred groups and some students have concerns about their own creative skills, they are keen to achieve the activity and enjoy having ‘real live’ children to work with. Parents of children have given their permission and are present throughout the assessment

The students seemed to understand the theory of play and were able to reflect on their own experiences of play as a child, however they did not have a good understanding of the benefits of learning through play. They also struggled with the concept of play spaces, therefore the tutor felt the best way to tackle this was for the students to ‘have a go’ and also have a project where they were able to reflect on their work as a team, their reflective practice and their model building skills. Students will have the opportunity to go on placement this academic year and need to develop a number of skills to be placement ready.

Task

The goals of activity were for students to:

  • Plan a play space with peers
  • Decide which resources to use
  • Decide the learning opportunities available for children in the early years up to 7yrs
  • Improve team working skills
  • Improve analytical skills
  • improve evaluation skills and listen to constructive criticism from peers and child

Actions

I informed the students of what they were going to do, you will go to a room which has a selection of resources, you will design a play space, you have 1 hour, and a child of 7 years will come with his parent and assess your play space, for learning capacity and play capacity.

You will be expected to work as a team, plan, assess, analyse and critique your play space

Obstacles

Some student were not confident to get involved with the project and let others take the lead, the lead people ensured that tasks were shared out equally in all but one group where one student watch the rest of her group rather than join in. difficulty for the tutor was to capture this and encourage collaborative working as the activity was split between 4 rooms. Some student became competitive and wanted to know from the tutor which was the best play space.

Results

The child enjoyed all of the play spaces and the tutor was pleased to see the variety of learning taking place by the students. All students had implemented activities for learning maths and English within their play space, so for example, giving opportunities for mark making. Most groups worked well and planned their activity. The students analysed their play spaces and evaluated what they could improve on, they also had an opportunity to see other group spaces and give constructive feedback to other groups.
