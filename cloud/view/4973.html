As I write, U101 is about to start it's 2nd year of presentation and my new student allocations have just come through. Apart from the excitement of starting another year, there are some housekeeping tasks that have to be done and, with #LAK11 in full swing, it seemed very appropriate to reflect on this.

The big question, on Course Level Analytics, is "if you could measure anything about your students, what information would you like?"

Extra content

At the start of every course I, like many other teachers, lecturers, and ALs, look through my student allocation quite carefully. I am trying to get to 'know' my students from the simple information they provide about themselves and, in particular, trying to identify any students that may require additional support, have particular needs, or who are in an especially high risk category.

I do this based (mainly) on 3 things :

  1. My institution's excellent documentation on basic pedagogy and student support;
  2. My experience from previous teaching;
  3. My gut reactions and feelings.
For example, it is known that the previous level of educational experience will have an influence on the general ability of the student to cope with an Open University course. Students with less experience will often (but not always) require a little bit of extra support and encouragement. This is useful information to know.
So, at the start of the course I do a quick risk analysis based on 4 main criteria :
  1. Whether the student is new to university / Open University;
  2. The age of the student;
  3. The educational level of the student;
  4. A mixture of other additional information I may (or may not) have about the student.
This then lets me target those students that I think will require additional support and make contact with them to check that I've got it right (this last bit is quite important, I think).
But what other start of course KPI's or criteria can we (measurably) use to help teachers and students to get off to a flying start?
