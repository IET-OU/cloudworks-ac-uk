A Quality Assurance Model for Student-Centered Learning and Teaching in Higher Education
Dr. Myunghee Ju Kang（Chair, Educational Technology Department, Ewha Womans University, Korea）

Extra content

Live blog of presentation

Quality assurance of student-centered learning

Myunghee Kan, Ewha Womans University, Korea

World’s largest women’s university

Will outline the quality assurance model they are using in their university to support student-centered learning (SCL)

Outline

  • Student-centered learning – why, what and how
  • Quality assurance of SCL – why, what and how

Why SCL?

  • Knowledge-based society – knowledge has to be actively generated, learners are different from the previous generation, the world is changing by knowledge, globalization is everywhere
  • Technology-based society – ubiquitous, wired/wireless, transparent, user friendly, web 2.0/web 3.0
  • Learning theories – constructivism, constructionism, generative learning, social learning, mindful learning
  • Teaching strategies – goal-based scenarios, project-based learning, problem-based learning, case-based reasoning, collaborative learning

What is SCL

  • Students are at the centre of the learning process, choice in what, how, when and why? (but a lot of students struggle with this)
  • Students are responsible for their learning – for their learning outcomes and processes (but if they are not mature enough they don’t want to do this)
  • Students are emerged into the learning environment – SCL i) content, ii) engagement, iii) interactions, iv) assessment, v) instructional strategies, vi) environment

So many professors are finding it difficult to make this transition to this approach – complaints from BOTH the students and the professors!

How SCL is practiced in Korea – efficiency and effectiveness is important – competitive exams, private institutes, private tutors

SCL practices in HE: Problem-based learning, Case-based learning, Goal-based Learning, Web-based Instruction, Communities of Practice, Learner Community, Virtual Classroom

Many teachers are questioning whether these approaches are the right way to do things, is this effective and efficient – therefore developed a QA model

Quality Assurance of ICT-based CL

Before:

  • Goal orientation (An individuals set of beliefs that reflect the reasons why they approach and engage in academic tasks - Eccles and Wigfield, 2002 – mastery approach/avoidance goal,
  • Performance approach/avoidance goal, work avoidance goal and social goal), task value (students perception non hos interesting, important and useful the task is – Pintrich et al. 1991 – interesting/useful/important)
  • Attribution – students attribute success or failure f their outcomes to factos such as ability, effort task difficutly and luck – Zimmerman and Shunk,2 007 – internal/external, stable/unstable, controllable/uncontrollable, causal attribution/expectancy change/emotional responses) and
  • Self-efficacy: judgments of personal capabilityes to organise and execute courses of action required to attain designed types of goals – Ba ndura, 1997 – Goal setting, effort investment, persistence in face of barriers, recovery from setbacks.

During: Cognitive presence, Emotional presence and social presence = engaged learning plus teaching presence plus telepresence

  • Telepresence – a set of technologies which allow a person to feel as if they were present to give the appearance that they were present or to have an effect at a location other than their true location– visual presence, sound presence, manipulation presence
  • Teaching presence – the instructional design and implementation which provide the perception of learning authenticity – activities, tasks, resources and assessment
  • Learning presence – human perceptions and responses in the context of learning in cognitive (understanding content, constructing knowledge and managing learning resources), emotional (perception, expression and management) and social dimensions (co-presence, influence, cohesiveness)

After: Satisfaction, achievement, transfer and transformation

  • Satisfaction, Achievement, Transfer (actual change of students attitude), and Transform (motivated/empowered/proactive)

Conclusion

  • SCL is a good strategy for us in a changing world
  • Student freedom and choice good but they need to be well prepared
  • Need to monitor the learning quality
  • Indicators for assuring the learning have to be installed during SCL
  • Automatic feedback system has to be equipped to guide students and teachers
