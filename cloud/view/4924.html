ohh goodie some critical comments to engage with!

Well I think it is evident that we need something to help teachers better use technologies and rethink the design of learning interventions. Evidence from the work that we have done suggest such design languages do help - make things more explicit and shareable and help them to be more critically reflective. Too often design focuses on content, design languages help teachers take a more holistic approach and consider all the facets of design. Also comparing design in teaching with design in other disciplines such as architecture and music it is evident from these disciplines that design languages have a number of advantages.

The idea of a language to solve a specific problem is tried and trusted area. The issue is that language design incorporates three aspects:

- abstract syntax

- concrete syntax

- semantics

The semantics tends to get neglected and or people confuse precision with semantics so they think adding OCL for example will provide semantics. Ways of adding semantics are:

  • Translational: Translating from concepts in one language into concepts in another language that have a precise semantics.
  • Operational: Modelling the operational behaviour of language concepts.
  • Extensional Extending the semantics of existing language concepts.
  • Denotational: Modelling the mapping to semantic domain concepts.

What works best for languages for learning design?
