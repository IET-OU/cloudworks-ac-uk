Peer Review & Assessment

  • Favourite
  • 477 views

Cloud created by:

Kylie Matthews
16 December 2015

Date: Tuesday 12 January 2016

Time: 10:00 - 12:00

Venue: Library Seminar Rooms 1&2

Programme

  • 10:00-10:30 - Peer Review in e-assessment on SD815: the first cohorts of students in Science - Payam Rezaie & Nick Adams (Science) 

This talk will focus on four key aspects:

        1. why peer learning is used on the module
        2. the design of the task and how it feeds forward to summative assessment
        3. managing the peer review process using Moodle Workshop on the VLE
        4.  benefits to students
  • 10:30-11:00 - L161 - Exploring languages and cultures - first steps in peer assessment - Annette Duensing & María Fernández-Toro (FELS)

In this session Annette & María will present an online activity designed to introduce level 1 students to peer review. It was piloted in 14J with a group of L161 tutors assuming the role of imaginary students, and their feedback was then used to develop an improved version of the activity. The revised version is now available to the 15J student cohort on the VLE. This online activity is currently in progress and a summary of the student's contributions to date will be presented for discussion. 

  • 11:00-11:30 - Peer review and Peer Assessment; how useful is the Moodle workshop tool - Peter Taylor (Science)

In this talk, Peter will review the usefulness of the workshop tool for peer review and peer assessment based on a series of pilot studies across a range of subjects and levels. The weakness of the tool and possible improvements for the future will also be discussed.

  • 11:30-12:00 - Coffee and Discussion: "Challenges of peer review and peer assessment"
