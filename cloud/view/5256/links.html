Fred Litto

Brazil has half the area and population of S America, 7th largest economy of the world

largest exporter of beef, fowl, sugar, coffee, and soy bean. 99% income tax returned completed online. At end of 2010 1 cell phone in operation for every citizen. Penetration of TV is 95%. Only 40% of residences have municipality provided water sanitation. Compete with Italy for having the highest cost of electricity. Pay ten times more for broadband than others.

Over half of primary school dont complete and only half of those in high school finish. Brazil’s 15 yr olds score v badly on PISA

Only 17% college students involved in study of math, science and engineering

 

Lots of creativity in art etc. Wacky telephone booths, but little space for creativity in Education, result of influence of Portuguese culture - very conservative and somewhat anti-intellectual. No books etc between 1500 - 1800!

1808 4 institutions of HE established, first university 1934. Legacy of laws and bureaucracy. Paternalistic state. 

 

Education never was a priority in the Luso-Brazilian Culture, creativity and innovation is prevented. Super-centralised power of ministry of education. Excess of rules and laws. Updating educational methods is difficult and too slow for society’s needs.

 

Paulo Freire - The pedagogy of the oppressed

Major effect of conservative thought generally on learning and then applying what you’ve learnt. No respect for dissidence, pressure on individual to avoid the road not taken, nor entrepreneurship and no debating societies in schools or colleges

 

My wish list: 

Bi-annual educational creativity index like PISA

Produce new materials for professors or students 

Main obstacle to greater creativity in education are the faculties of education where future schoolteachers are taught

1992 Young people and their organisation to the mass media - an international study - Bradley Greenburg, Michigan State University
