Keynote: Conole Embracing change, where might daring to think differently take us?

  • Favourite
  • 1080 views

Massey University, 10th -14th December 2009

Cloud created by:

Gráinne Conole
9 December 2009

 Abstract

In this talk I give an overview of the context in which education is now operating and in particular focus on a set of paradoxes thatarise as a result of new technologies and the consequential educational dilemmas that result. It draws on international research including work that has looked at students' use of technologies. I suggest that there is a gap between the potential of technologies ( and how they might be used to support learning) and their use in reality. I suggest we need to think differently and present some of the work we have been doing on designing for learning. I conclude with a framework for intervention and a set of grand challenges for the future.

A paper going into more depth about some of the technology paradoxes and associated educational dilemmas  (associated with a keynote at the Ascilte conference) can be found here. 

Questions

  • Students increasingly digital – demands on institutions?
  • Students and teachers - personalised environment of tools vs. institutional tools?
  • What new forms of blended learning spaces are needed?
  • How do we support  new approaches to design and delivery of courses to make more effective use of technologies and lead to an enhance student learning experience?
  • How do we take account of a digital divide that is ever narrower but deeper?
  • What new digital literacy skills will learners and teachers need ?
  • What new pedagogical models are needed to marry the affordances of personalisation with the best affordances of technologies?
  • How do we account for blurring boundaries (real/virtual, formal/informal, etc)?

Grand challenges

  • Digital divide is ever increasing. How do we deal with this?
  • Are we seeing Jenkin’s 12 digital literacies skills? How can we help those in education develop these more?
  • How can we study these complex, fast evolving technological systems ? New methodologies?
  • What theoretical insights should we draw on?
  • Is there evidence of new pedagogies emerging?
