Programme

 Training educators: building capacity through open courses. 

  • 10:00 - 10:50 'Learning to (Re)-Use Open Educational Resources: design principles and localisation' - Allison Littlejohn, IET and Hélène Pulker, Department of Languages, FELS

The ExplOERer project is fostering sustainability of open educational resources (OER) in higher education (https://exploerer.wordpress.com/ ). An important goal is to provide professional learning opportunities supporting the reuse and repurposing of OER in new contexts. To achieve this goal the project is focusing on supporting adult educators to more consistently and effectively reuse and repurpose of OER in and for their professional practice.

An important aspect of this goal is to consider how professional learning is structured. Guidelines were developed to structure professional learning opportunities relevant to educators’ open educational resource (OER) engagement. These guidelines provide advice to facilitate the design and construction of professional learning opportunities to support educators in building new learning practices around OER. In determining how best to support educators’ learning with and from OER, it was necessary to consider not only the nature and structure of learning opportunities they require but also the knowledge and content these opportunities should encompass.

This talk will present the six discreet areas of knowledge that need to be targeted and that are proposed by the guidelines. It will also present how the guidelines were integrated in the design of the open course ‘Learning how to (re)use OER’, output of the ExplOERer project. Finally, there will be opportunity for discussing ideas around the design of online courses for professional learning.

  • 10:50 - 11:10 Coffee break
  • 11:10 - 12:00 'Building capacity in teacher education with the TESS-India MOOC' - Kris Stutchbury, Department of Education, FELS

As part of the TESS-India project, we designed and ran a MOOC: Enhancing Teacher Education through OER. The MOOC was part of our capacity building strategy for teacher educators and ran twice during 2015 using a ‘hybrid’ model with high levels of face-to-face support. There were 10,000 registrations across the two presentations and a completion rate of 51%. The evidence from India is that the unique design of the course alongside formal and informal mechanisms of support, together, provided a unique opportunity for project participants to experience the pedagogy that we are trying to promote. We are still exploring the overall impact on the project, but we believe that MOOCs has made a significant impact in ways that we expected, and in some that we did not.

This talk will explore the reasons for the success of the MOOC and the implications for the future, both in terms of educational development projects but also for work closer to home.
