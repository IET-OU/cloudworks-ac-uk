This Cloud collates the feedback from both the UK and Cyprus 'Using technologies in teaching' workshops. Thank you to you all for completing the evaluation forms - I have collated the feedback here. If there is anything you'd like to add do so below in the discussion section. We'd be happy to hear from you :-)

Extra content

Feedback from the UK workshop held on 17th September 2010

Q1. Have your objectives for the workshop been met (what were these)?

  • Shared knowledge - yes
  • a. learn about/ experience programmespreviously unused
  • b. Meet/ swap stories with other PGCE students
  • c. Contrast/ compare subject or ICT knowledge with other students
  • Comparing four web 2.0 tools was used to help expand our horizons regarding how to get the most out of technology in teaching
  • I wasn't sure what my objectives were but I have found it very useful and interesting
  • Yes - to learn more about technologies in teaching and different tools available
  • Yes
  • We weren't really sure what to expect
  • Unsure of what the course would be about
  • (was hoping for some instruction on using white/smart board)

Q2. What 3 words would you use to describe Cloudworks?

  • Interactivity, communication, creativity
  • Contemporary, worth investigating...
  • Interactive, community...
  • Collaborative, user friendly, informative
  • Potentially useful, more investigation required

Q3. What 3 words would you use to describe the CompendiumLD notation?

  • Good overview of lessons
  • Reflective practice
  • Useful, thought proviking, comprehensive
  • Art on it's own, connectivity, vision, fun,
  • Interesting, useful for working with collegues
  • Fun, thinking outside of the 'box'
  • Difficult, confusing, usefiul
  • Nice, not necessary
  • Thought provoking, motivating, inspiring

Q4. What did you like about the format of the workshop?

  • Clear, straight forward, informative
  • Informality, friendly
  • Clear and easily interactive
  • Friendly/ approachable
  • Useful to have other 'extra' presenters to ask/ promote discussion
  • Broken down activities and laptops on the tables
  • Breaks so not 'head heavy'
  • Excellent content. well suited to all (ie worked well for PGCEs in Maths and MFL...)
  • Small steps towards our presentation and gradual development
  • The group work in planning a lesson together - also very valuable having subject leaders to help in this process
  • Supportive, fun, interactive, social
  • The lesson planning session using notation - a very visual way.
  • Practical, collaborative, lots of talking, subject specific tables
  • The freedom to express negative, as well as positive thoughts and opinions
  • Vey interactive
  • Good pace

Q5. What didn't you like or could be improved?

  • We had a small table!
  • We would really like practical, subject specific, music technology training
  • Maybe offer to do some initial reading/ preparation first
  • Wasn't convinced that the tools we were given were of benefit to the lessons we were trying to plan - didn't really understand the technology!
  • It would be good if we had more knowledge of what is achievable with the technology
  • During the week - difficult if one is on a placement
  • I liked Rebecca;s idea of giving subject specific ideas as a starting point, especially for music!
  • Too noisy discussion, laptop screens too small
  • Slightly larger font

Q6. What action points have you identified as a result of this workshop?

  • To always evaluate usefulness of resources
  • Importance of learning objectives
  • White boards at both ends, when group work [to provide] seperate space
  • Find out more about all types/ styles of ICT support - but use it wisely
  • Familiarize ourselves with new programmes - excellent workshop!
  • Working on planning in a different way; using all possible interactive tools.
  • Learn about the 4 interactive tools: prepare to integrate in PGCE experiences and beyond.
  • To evaluate why I shoulkd use a particular technology - whether this is high-tech or just pen and paper.
  • How to plan a lesson using collaboration and non-linear planning.
  • I felt this too [with regards to non-linear planning]
  • Remain open-minded!
  • Open minded when using technology in lessons.

Feedback from the Cyprus workshop Sept 10

Q1. Have your objectives for the workshop been met (what were these?)

 Learning design – We got some innovative ideas about designing and organising lessons with the implementation of new technology

Yes, though we would like to have further time to explore the tools used in the workshop. The aim: to see how ICT comes into the lesson through a design model

Obj: How to design courses using ICT        Met: Yes to a high degree

Not sure, not very clear

Partly, yes. Using learning design theory to create technology-infused lessons

Q2. What 3 words would you use to describe Cloudworks?

Useful – information sharing – ambitious

Share – find - discuss

Informative – Intuitive – Interactive

Did not have an opportunity to explore Cloudworks

Communication - sharing – community

Interesting – collaborative - engaging

Q3. What 3 words would you use to describe the CompendiumLD notation?

Flexible – creative - communicative

Plan - reflect

Unnecessary – confusing

Visualisation-communicative – dynamic

Innovative – throught-provoking – flow

Easy to understand – helpful for organising a lesson plan – all-encompassing

Q4. What did you like about the format of the workshop?

Interaction with colleagues, collaboration, discussions, building knowledge by search and discussion.

Not “heavy” and well structured

Liked. Not boring.

Hands-on, collaboration, interaction

Interaction with team members

Information about new tools (tools that we didn’t know)

Heard about the current state of learning design

Heard about the current state of the educational system

Flexibility

Diversity of opinions

Q5. What didn’t you like or could be improved?

Each group could present their learning design procedure more systematically to all other groups (e.g. jigsaw groups formed at the end)

Knowing the tools before

Nothing

There was not enough time to become familiar with the tools

We did not announce group’s results

Lack of feedback after each activity (need to share as a whole after each activity)

Instructions for some activities were not very clear

Hands on practice with the web 2.0 tools or demonstration of the basic features of the 4 web 2.0 tools 

Q6. What action points have you identified as a result of this workshop?

A new process of lesson planning

Macro- and meso- level

Communication vs interactivity

Better course planning which will include the web 2.0 tools

Planning in a new format

Reconceptualisation  of the way technology is to be used in each of the lesson plan phases

Feedback from the June 2011 UK workshop

1. Have your objectives for the workshop been met (what were these?)

  • Curiosity & open mind - MET
  • Learn more about Cloudworks and teh project - MET (better understanding of what is being done)
  • To learn more about available tools and methods - MET
  • To find out more - YES
  • To some extent
  • Should have more time to complete activities
  • Good organisation, feedback
  • Came with open mind - YES
  • To learn more about LD - YES
  • (As above) - YES
  • Yes, learnt a lot about course design principles. Feel able to apply these to current knowledge and experience and enhance development skills
  • I feel a lot more confident about learning design now
  • Feels helpful to have some appropriate terminology

2. What 3 words would you use to describe Cloudworks?

  • Confusing (a bit), useful, good, innovative
  • Interesting and novel technology in use
  • Intangible, exciting, the future maybe
  • Thought provoking, somewhat challenging, possibility?
  • Cumbersome (navigation problems)
  • Potential
  • Informative
  • Not sure yet! (x2)
  • Enabling theory into practice

3. What 3 words would you use to describe the CompendiumLD notation?

  • Pass - not looked
  • Useful, very visual and concise, need to know how to use more
  • Open to interpretation!
  • Fairly useful
  • Helps focus and structure
  • Fun with stickers
  • Pleased to see designs were organic in other groups too

4. What did you like about the format of the workshop?

  • Small groups - working with great colleagues
  • Using new tools
  • Cutting out and colouring
  • Positive collaborative working
  • Not boring
  • Plenty of variety, challenging but good structure
  • Opening up new avenues, ways of thinking about teaching and learning
  • Time to discuss
  • Having practical things to focus on and discussions
  • Good mix of activity and discussion
  • made you think
  • Very practical
  • Pleanty of discussion and participation

5. What didn't you like or could be improved?

  • Flip chart at the front, not much else
  • Have workshop earlier - it would frame and help with online (Cloudworks) activity
  • Finding my way to the Michael Young Building
  • Screensize (of Smartboard) too small. Laptops helped but 4 watching them was difficult
  • Nothing springs to mind - enjoyed it :-)
  • Computer each or recomend bring own
  • Wall screen bit dificult to see

6. What action points have you identified as a result of this workshop?

  • Try and document my own learning design
  • Try and use tools
  • Uploading photo's on Flickr
  • Feedback experience of workshop to module team
  • Would like to know how our responses are going to be implemented/incorporated into teh package
  • Focus on follow up!
  • Do my homework!
  • To look at my implicit designing of tutorials to a ld approach i.e. from a students perspective
  • To share what I have learnt back at my region and to try to apply this for TU100
  • Work together with other cluster group tutors
  • Apply to next tutorial
  • Review my tutorials for next presentation start
  • Will use mapping notation to design Elluminate activities to share with other tutors
  • Will revisit earlier Cloudworks study in the light of improved knowledge
