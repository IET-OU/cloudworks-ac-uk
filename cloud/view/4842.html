Patrick McAndrew
16 November 2010

The opening remarks by Gennady BORDOVSKY were in Russian – but with simultaneous translation. His introduction led into UNESCO medals to Dendev Badarch and two colleagues.

 

Dendev switched to English to welcome us to St Petersburg and then back to Russian. He outlined the creation of UNESCO chairs in the area aof ICT in Education as one of the key actions. There is a theme of openness to one of the strands of the conference in order to address the limitations caused by copyright. UNESCO views education as one of the “rights of the world” and the way to build a knowledge society.

 

Bernard Cornu spoke on behalf of the governing body to thank those who have invited us here and organised. The role of IITE in supporting the use of ICT in accessing, learning and developing education and knowledge. This involves technical, pedagogical and political questions – all of which fit with the mission of IITE. IITE has been revitalised leading to this event, a range of publications and activities.

 

Representative of the Russian Federation: the need to involve all in education and research. Created the Russian Analytic approach involving competition for grants and prizes that link research and education.  Problems remain of access, use and quality. Education resources need to be high quality and the structure and capabilities of those involved in the conference can help address these.

State rep for UNESCO. There is a large programme to provide access to online learning for schools. This needs to be backed up by development of teacher capability and resources. The plan for 2012/13 has 2 year priority to promote ICT access with a remit for access for all. There are also linked meetings and actions on sustainable development.
