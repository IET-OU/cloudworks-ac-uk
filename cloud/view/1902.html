  • Broader context research/teaching nexus and where inquiry-based learning sits in that
  • How might we better strengthen the link between research and teaching in the student experience?
  • Making design for learning more explicit in student's practice not just teacher's practice
  • Starting point: quote from Boyer Report (1998) 'The ecology of a university depends on a deep and abiding understanding that inquiry, investigation and discovery are the heart of the enterprise, whether in funded research projects or in undergraduate classoroom or graduate apprenticeships. Everyone a a unvieristy should be a discoverer, a lerner. That shared mission binds together all that happens on a campus,. The teaching responsibility of the university is to make all its students particpants in the mission. 
  • Not new idea, links back to von Humboldt, 1810. Unviersity shouldl treat learning always as consisting of not yet wholly solved problems and hence always in research mode.
  • Theme in policty and debate in 20th century. Conceptions of what a university might be. Angela Brew, 2006. Inquiry centre stage. Ron Barneet, pedagogy of joint discovery
  • Lots going on in terms of intiatives internationally looking at relationship between research and teaching
  • Paul Ramsden, Chief Exec of HEA about future of higher educated emphasised research-based study in the undergraduate curriculum. Boyer report about reforming practice of research universities, not about mainstreaming across the whole sector
  • What mean by Inquiry Based Learning at University of Sheffield. Pedagogical approaches organised around process of inquiry, distinguish from those where by-product or add-on. really putting student experience of inquiry at the centre. About student developing own capacity to create own knowledge, investigating authentic, often open-ended questions, adopting the practices of scholarship or research, exploring a knowledge base actively and potentialy contribute new knowledge. Move from information-oriented mode to inquiry to discovery-orientated mode. Partnerships between students and staff, building staff in as co-learner. Support for inquiry, guidance on relvant inquiry methods including how to frame good questions, Support for development of information literacty, critical thinking, self-reflection and other capabilities associated with productive inquiry. Creates opportunities for students to share teh results of their inquiries with peers and others.  Research shows design implicit in colleague's practice
  • Conceptualise design for IBL - CILASS (2009) Model of the inquiry process by Justice et al. Not linear, rather messy, spiral. How do we represent these? Questions about relationship between student inquiry and Web 2.0. Affinity at conceptual level between values of both. Might dissolve distinction between producer and produced-for, the designer and designed-for. Asking questions about whether or not can assumes students are digital natives in this kind of context. Research questioning whether students skilled to effectively support own learning through Web 2.0 tools. How to support students develop their skills. Help students choose tools to create own PLE
  • Simple conceptual framework Levy (2009) Two axes - student-framed vs. tutor-framed and information inquiry vs discovery inquiry Pursuing (what is the existing answer to my question?), Authoring (how can I answer my question?) Identifying (what is the existing answer to this question?), Producing in the quadranets (how can I answer this question?) cbl, pbl in bottom left, orginal research on righthand side
  • Design for learning as relevant for students as educators.
  • Issues and challenges - large student cohorts, 'high impact' activites right from beginning at levels 0 and 1, research has show how powerful for students to have small-scale structured opportunites from level 1, supporting transition, support for information and digital literacy, connecting students inquiries to scholarly and research activity, circulating exemplars of student-led designs
  • DESILA - exploring LAMS for IBL. Response mixed - version looked at invited design for linearity, not in principle that other modes possible, perceived affordances to do with linearity and teacher controller. (using LAMS v. 1)
  • Design tools for IBL?build in high level of flexibility (constraint vs. freedom), considering four IBL modes, learn from research in field of computer-supported inquiry learning, build in opportunity for pedagogical reflection, access to exemplar design
  • Pilot user - like condustin and orchestra, learn how to orchestrate less.
