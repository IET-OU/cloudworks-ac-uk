Title Muddling through Moodle Management

Narrator

I design and setup elearning solutions for small healthcare agencies who want their staff to do compliance training online.  The key to getting this setup to work is to make sure the Training Administrator (TA) who manages staff training, is confident in the use of the new system.  If the TA understands and instructs her staff well, then I am not bothered by support calls.

Situation

The TA usually has had a the job of following protocols with regards training requirements  and ensuring that training courses are organised and staff files are kept current.   A new system of work can cause changes to the established way of working, and needs to be introduced with care.  If the online training solution is seen to make everyone’s lives easier and more manageable, then it is continued by management.  If there is bad feedback from staff, then it is discontinued.

I have found that a training that follows specific protocols works well in these situations.  I also have found that the training must take place with the candidate’s own computer, so that they can take ownership of the training directly.

Task

If the client understands how to enrol students, what the common FAQ’s are, and how to deal with common problems, then I do not get inundated with support queries.  The measure of success is measured over the ensuing weeks, by the number of support calls I receive, and by the feedback the system receives from staff.

Actions

The first action was ensuring that the candidate’s computer was setup in a way that ensured a successful  outcome.

The company IT department had left IE7 as the default web browser.  This is so out of date, that it wouldn’t work with  the HTML5  coursework that we use.  I had to ask for an upgrade, and preferably for google chrome (which is automatically updated) to be installed. 

The next stage was to set up a series of steps that the candidate could follow for each of the processes involved.

These included templates of instructions for enrolment, what to do when emails went missing, how to print out certificates and the basics of managing the site.

By having a folder with protocols set up, the training was reasonably simple and straightforward.

 I also asked the TA to take notes that she could refer to in the future.

 

 

Results

The results of the training were better than expected. By focusing on the client’s desire to follow systems, I was able, in a short space of time, to get the online system rolled out to the agency’s staff.

In the first week or so there were some minor hiccoughs, but things went relatively smoothly.

With my time not taken up doing support, I was able to offer some further courses to the company that they could use for staff development.

 Both staff and management were very supportive of the system as both groups felt it gave them greater flexibility, while increasing interaction within the company.  I felt that this was due, to a large degree, to the confidence that everyone had in using the system.  This confidence was hinged on the clear instructions and support that they received at the start of their training by the TA.  While the TA’s attitude and personal competence played a major part, I would like to think that the training she received at the beginning, also helped in the success.

Reflections

Understanding that the TA wanted a series of protocols to follow, and providing these in a clear way, underpinned the success of this training.  She was not interested in the the pedagogal and instructional theories that underpinned the training.  The TA wanted to give clear instructions to candidates in what to do to complete the training, and wanted a simple outcome at the end of each training session.

Understanding  the TA’s objectives, and gearing the training to both her objectives, and her existing way of working, helped make the transition smooth.
