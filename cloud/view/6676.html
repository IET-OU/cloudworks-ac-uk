Prototyping the addition of summative assessment methods to the PPC

  • Favourite
  • 731 views

Cloud created by:

Diana Laurilllard
1 December 2012

Thinking about how best to add in a summative testing activity to the PPC I would classify it as 'learning through production', so it does not need an addition to the drop-down menu. However, unlike other activities it needs additional descriptors, such as 'rubric', 'marking scheme', etc. the details of which should be available as part of the design. So I'm beginning to look at how teachers want to do this by getting them to specify how they know what their students have learned by the end of a session, and watch what they do. No need to prototype anything new, just use the tool as a prototyping format, in which teachers use the different ways of editing to express what they want to put in.

I'd use think-aloud plus stimulated recall and interview, for three teachers, and then review what that suggests about the next step to take.

There would also be additional resources (tests, questions, etc) that need classifying according to the type of assessment being carried out. Those would emerge from the observations of the three teachers - but there would be lots more to add from further investigations. But this would be a basis to start thinking about the design.

Good question. Could be either. But the teacher needs to know how well they do, whether it is teacher marked or computer marked. But not sure what you mean by self assess. They can do that if they have a model answer, or the right answer, against which to compare their own. Otherwise it would have to be computer marked. The more rapid the feedback the better, which is the advantage of computer marked. But this would be a way to design and evaluate all types of design for summative asst. I would like to find out what more features teachers would like in the PPC.
