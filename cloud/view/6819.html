Designing for Self-Regulated Learning

Cloud created by:

Donatella Persico
9 January 2013

My contribution to the ARV TILD workshop is entitled “Towards a map of existing representations for learning design” and can be found at the above link.

However, I would like to add that I am also interested in Self-Regulated Learning (SRL) and, specifically, in the way it can be supported within technological environments. In particular, I am interested in Leaning Design principles to support Self-Regulated Learning. What do I mean by this?

Self-regulated learners are individuals who actively and consciously control their own learning from the cognitive, metacognitive, behavioural, emotional, and motivational points of view. This ability entails a set of interdisciplinary competences that greatly improve the learning process and are essential to cope with the challenges of life-long-learning in the knowledge society. A well-known, consolidated model of SRL describes SRL as a cyclic process consisting of (1) forethought, (2) performance or volitional control, and (3) self-reflection (Zimmerman, 2000).

Research into SRL has shown that there are methods to support the development of SRL, and that Technology Enhanced Learning Environments (TELEs) have a potential for this, provided that they offer learners opportunities to practice self-regulation (Azevedo, 2005; Azevedo & Hadwin, 2005; Dettori & Persico, 2011; Steffens, 2006; Delfino, Dettori & Persico, 2010; Dabbagh N. & Kitsantas, 2004; Beishuizen, Carneiro, & Steffens, 2007). In order to make sure that such opportunities are provided (and hopefully taken advantage of!), those who design for learning should make sure that the TELE  has suitable features. Examples of these features have already been identified: the TELE should allow for personalized learning, it should explicitly support planning, self-monitoring and metacognition, it should provide opportunities for self-assessment.  All in all, it should allow for students to make choices, so that they get used to take decisions about their own learning and, ultimately, control  their own learning.  

To conclude, I would be interested in joining a group aiming to discuss design principles for SRL and possibly put forward a project proposal that considers these aspects
