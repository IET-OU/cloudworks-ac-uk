E-Textbooks 

Martin Hallik, University of Tartu

 

 

HE is becoming international, English is used more and more, but important to keep preserve Estonian as a language. We need to find the right balance. Have linguistic principles at the university of Tartu. Lack Estonian language text books in many subjects. Estonia has a very small market, cost is high and number of books published is small. There is a national programme of HE textbooks, annually 50 textbooks are financed. Motivation is an important factor in terms of encouraging faculty to write textbooks. Universities need to value the contribution of teachers.  

 

E-Textbooks offer a solution to the lack of textbooks and they are coming more popular. They can help to save costs compared with paper textbooks. E-textbooks are also funded by the national programme. Easier to correct errors with e-books. Have created an e-brary database and also DSpace. What is an e-textbook? Includes content, learning objectives, guidelines, activities and links. You can search within the text, can mark or group the text, include multimedia. Their e-books are in PDF format. Can include electronic bookmark, hyperlinks, test presentations, slide presentations, online forums, graphs, tables, animations and simulation. An e-textbook is free for students. They can be easily accessed from anywhere. Can convert into an audio file so that students can listen to them. They offer courses on how to write e-textbooks. An issue can be difficulty reading from a screen requires 25% more effort.
