The Impossible Open Education Future

27 October 2011

Philipp Schmidt
Executive Director and Co-Founder, Peer 2 Peer University

P2Pu has a long history with Open Ed.  P2Pu is an open community to support education which adds a social layer to OER and enables learners through a 'bottom up' approach.

  • 23,000 accounts
  • 4,500 - 6,000 users per month

P2Pu explores the possibility of a 'self-learner' model of education, and works with other organisations within the OER movement.  Schmidt advocates being more radical and more ambitious.

  • OER cost savings are a big deal
  • Being 'open' is not a quick fix for all the challenges in a broken system
  • Disruption comes from the fringes
  • OER should retain the feeling of a 'lab'
  • It's not about education as a whole
  • Open spaces are serendipitous and unpredictable
  • Good facilitation doesn't (necessarily) scale well
  • For larger courses, it's better to think of a community of learners
  • P2Pu understands learning as 'making things with others'

But what about assessment?  Is it an art or a science?  Is it about intituion or analytics?  People often place too much emphasis on statistics, or look at the wrong statistics.  Schmidt feels that we place too much emphasis on some statistics, such as SATs.  Authentic assessment (i.e. part of the activity) is easier to defend.  Quick feedback is also important, as is 'good failure' (without stigma) which leads to more motivation.
If learning is making things with others, then what is assessment?  We are only at the earliest stages of a successful analytics... we don't necessarily know what to look for, but we are starting to collect data.  Showcasing work and portfolios online is something that can be built into the learning experience.  If learning is social, it's important to listen to the community.

Badges can act as motivators and guides.  They are transparent (meaning that their evidence base is accessible) and promote experimentation and innovation.
