Exploring the possibility of developing a student-centered learning systems for vocational skills development

Haruo Nishinosono, Institute for learning development

  • Aimed to construct his course around activities, with the aim of improving student engagement
  • Provided a useful historical reflection of some of the changing educational movements over the last thirty/forty years – such as the increasingly prominence given to lifelong learning
  • Range of learning opportunities – formal, non-formal and informal learning
  • How do we recognise these different forms of learning?
  • Discusses the modern context of education – globalisation, employability, increase in tuition feeds for HE
  • In comparison to other countries Japan has high fees and low percentage of students who get grants, in comparison US has higher comparative fees but also higher percentage of grants
  • Using IT we will be able to reduce the cost per head
  • France has an interesting model whereby they can keep fees low through partnership with industry. Similar model in the UK to support/promote professional/vocational training – for example there is an apprenticeship tax in the UK
  • Generation X goes to collage – Peter Sacks – translated in 2000, Weak attitude to learning with this generation
  • http://www.petersacks.org/
  • Review of the book: This is the incredible, amusing, horrifying, yet true story, of how the author, a journalist turned college professor, came face to face with Generation X: jaded, unachieving, highly demanding, yet lacking any respect for standards or intelligence. These scholars wore bored looks, ample attitudes, and reversed baseball caps. They expected to earn top grades by just showing up in class, which they often interrupted with their portable TVs, cellular phones, or personal pagers. In order to keep his job, the author had to give up old-fashioned educational goals in exchange for mindless titillation and coming across as a cool and unassuming guy. Partly as a wry experiment, Sacks decided to pursue this approach remorselessly, treating his twentysomething students like a kindergarten playgroup, and by so doing he became a spectacular "success" as a "teacher." http://www.opencourtbooks.com/books_n/generation_x.htm
  • Four classifications: Formal learning, semi-formal learning, non-formal learning and informal learning. These are different in different cultural context
  • Recognition of learning outcomes – these must be recognised. Inadequacy of the current educational model – based around contact hours. How can learning outcomes be recognised in other forms of learning, apart from formal learning.
  •  Need new models and types of institutions to address these new learning patterns
  • Important to create a coducive environment to suport student-centered learning
