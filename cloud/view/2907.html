Question

  • Concept of Total Place, being more agile within and beyond the University, making the most of innovative thinking, practice.

Chair: Professor Chris Brink, Newcastle University
Contributors: Sir Michael Bichard, Institute of Government; Dr Don Olcott, OBHE; Ed Smith, Demos

Extra content

Chris Brink - introduced the team gathered around the table. Including Sir Michael Bichard is soon to be Lord.

Engagement should be the "Third core function" alongside Research and Teaching/Learning. In the past there was a phase of looking at engagement as for the public good, then a second phase of third strand workign with business, and then a third phase of regional engagement.

However it is best to think of it as alongside reserach and teaching and learning - ways to bring out civic value from these. How can the univeirtisty function as an integral part of civic society: with regional, national and international engagement. Essential what differnce does your university make?

It is not enough to take a "we are so clever" position but rather see ways to learn. It is important to see how to address engagement within management structure.

Approach: Innovation, creativity, and agility. Regional, national, international.

Michael Bichard - previous work on being a "total place" i.e. delivering across a range. Aim to change the way people behave through leadership - not just a plan but a change.

Leading a place means going beyond the measures that are inward looking (e.g. REF) and to understand what we do as an agency in the community. Organisation then need to trust each other and build coalitions and colaborative behaviours. If people do a good job in this area then they deserve also to be recognised and rewarded.

Too much reorganisation leads to "poorer services at more cost". Instead a culure of innovation is needed that avoids being defensive - particularly to save real money in such things as buildings, IT etc. We must do better - e.g. only 1% of those looked after go on to HE, while 33% of those in prison have been. This is a huge contrast to Denmark - 60% go on to HE. So we can and must do better.

There are e.g.s of this being addressed but not across the piece.
