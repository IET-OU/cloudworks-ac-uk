Keynote – Chris Cobb, Pro Vice Chancellor, Roehampton University:
"It is not the strongest of the species that survives, nor the most intelligent, but rather the one most adaptable to change..."

Extra content

Chris Cobb, Pro Vice Chancellor at Roehampton University, London, talking about adaptability.

Clarence Darrow quotes. Actually he said "It is not the strongest of the species that survives ..." - not Darwin!

Also, "I have never killed a man, but I have many obituaries with great pleasure." (parents, children) ... "History repeats itself, and that's one of the things that's wrong with history."

Service oriented architecture (SOA), enterprise architecture. You shouldn't care where the services you use come from...

There are many types of university - collegiate, federated (distance!) ... a state of flux. All different - principal financial, HR, student systems in use in UK - graphs. A range of suppliers, no clear winner.

References to the "Mac initiative" - 1990s?

Woa - lots of acronyms and names - SAP, ERP, Tribal (product). Lots of add-ons - conferencing, Manchester merged 2 ERPs - which didn't work.

WPM (Birmingham University :) ) - online payment system, hosted - used by over half of UK universities(?), over 80 institutions. software as a service, on the cloud.

"The Princeton Review" - admissions system. It/ one of these systems manages qualification validation (interesting, Tony Hirst). UKPass, Grad Advantage.

"Campus M" - mobile applications. iPhone apps. - pastoral, use to find friends, navigate campus, borrow books - uses GPS.

Student Pad - online accomodation system - for landlords.

Surveys - eg. Electric Paper.

Catalogue searching, reading lists eg. TALIS Aspire, Aqua Browser.

Student email - Google/Microsoft.

VLE hosting, remote backup & storage ...

-- Key points: hosted, in the cloud, mix and match approach, you can be more agile, maybe.

C. Darrow, "History repeats itself, and that's one of the things that's wrong with history."

Eg. Universities are now clubbing together, again. We don't need "SOA"  (not about formal definitions).

A long quote, from "Painting the clouds", Educause review, vol 43, no 6 (Nov/dec 2008) Colin Currie, Director of Administrative..., Princeton University.

C.Darrow "I have sufferede from being misunderstood, but I would have suffered a hell of a lot more if I had been understood."

Audience question - paraphrasing, "should we be integrating/building new systems now? (cuts 20%?, merges), or should we wait for merges etc."

Chris - "don't wait." Too many cottage industries. 33% of UK's research is un-funded! Potential alarm bells for big funders and government.

  • Huge state of flux and change, big financial cut backs, 5-20% cut backs on back of the global credit crunch. We are also working in the context of a large review of HE policy, as well as speculation about contraction, about universities merging.
  • Move towards skills agenda, also speculation about returning to the divde of universities and polytechnic. At such times of change, technology can act as a catlyst. The technology as the burning platform, people have to jump
  • Enterprise architecture – way of enabling conversations with end users, way of linking business processes and services, which is what we should be concentrating on, not the actual technologies.
  • A review last year shows that there is a lot of variety across the sector.
  • UCISA survey annually shws a similar picture shows we are focused in on one or two suppliers, these days are numbered, this is likely to change. The MAC initiative didn’t work (where HEFCE asked universities to work together).

Going to start looking to different types of suppliers, smaller suppliers, who are delivering things in a different way. For example:

  • WPM, came out of Birmingham University, dealing with payments, a hosted system. Little to manage institutional, uses software as services. Over 80 institutions are using it.
  • Princeton review – area of p/g admissions which is outside of UCAS, there is no governmental shared services for this. Provides an online application form delivered to institutions, they do the marketing, transfer the information into student record systems and manage the reference handling etc. They are providing a hosted service.
  • Campus M - provides mobile apps which link into your core corporate systems. Sheffield are using this already. Utilises GPS technologies to help students find where their friends are, find where they are on the campus, alerts into the timetabling system. Again all hosted.
  • Student Pad – hosted environment where an institution can put in details about private landlords, university gets a fee from the landlord
  • HR recruitment – London school of economics, job application will call through a system called WCN
  • At Roehampton we are mapping out all our systems to review how to work towards a service orientated world
  • SCONUL is managing a project looking at library management systems
  • TALIS aspire - reading lists
  • Catalogue searchig eg Aqua Browser
  • Studetn email - google/microsoft
  • Federated authentication and authorisation
