eLearning Community Event: Data Insight and the work of IET Wranglers: Interpreting OU student learning data and understanding its shortcomings

The June eLC event, 'Data Insight and the work of IET Wranglers: Interpreting OU student learning data and understanding its shortcomings' will take place on Wednesday 26th June 2013 in the Jennie Lee Building Meeting Room 1 Ground Floor, from 10:00 to 12:00. 

Live link to broadcast:

The recording of the event will  be made available on Stadium. 

As always, we aim to stick to time, so please feel free to drop in and out according to your availability and interest.  

If you plan to attend, have any queries or wish to suggest a future event, please email IET-ELC-Admin.    

Information about this and other eLC events, together with slides, notes and/or recordings of previous events can be found on Cloudworks and theKnowledge Network. 

Date: 26 June 2013

Location: Jennie Lee Building Meeting Room 1 Ground Floor

Programme: 10:00 - 12:00

Audience : This session will be of interest to

  • people who are responsible for producing ‘learning design’ data,  and other curriculum data and inputting it to University systems- you will see how others make use of that data,
  • people who want to understand about what University data can tell us  about student use of, and satisfaction with, aspects of modules- we will share some of our data with, and explore its interpretation,
  • people who want to make use of the data and analysis produced by data Wranglers in our reports -  we will introduce some of the themes that are emerging across the faculties, and  point you to the more detailed reports that you can download later. 

Presenters : The session will be presented by a group of Wranglers including: Gill Kirkup, Robin Goodfellow, Doug Clow, Anne Jelfs, John Pettit and Linda Price 

Schedule:

10:00 - Coffee

10:30 - Introduction to Data Wrangling – its aim and activities. 

10:40- 11:40 - Emerging Themes

10:40 – 1. Student study activities – in print and online – how to understand student preference

11:00  - 2. Independent learning,  collaboration and forum use – how to understand student preferences

11:20 – 3. Student use of other tools – Elluminate 

11:40 – Challenges to using the data we have to understand student behaviour

12:00 - Close

If you would like to attend please e-mail IET-ELC-Admin@open.ac.uk
