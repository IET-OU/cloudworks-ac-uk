Tony Ackroyd: My dream: Learning design project proposal

A digital futures course

Tags:

Your situation (context)

A final year 12 week course for students studying Digital Media type degrees (eg computing, games, web, multimedia, mainly BSc)
At the University of Greenwich
Main actors: me as course coordinator, ~50 students as participants
3 hours a week in one block, one large classroom. A further hour where i am available but not timetabled.

The intention of the course is
  1. to prepare students for coping with self learning in a rapidly changing field.
  2. introduce some new areas that haven’t been covered in other courses
  3. reinforce ideas already come across (eg project management, academic writing, soft skills, professional ethos)


Assessments, at the moment , are
  1. an individual report on an  area of upcoming technology
  2. a group (4-7 persons)  pitch which is looking for funding  to build a product based on their combined research

Most of the students want a heavily structured course, and to be taught. They do not like learning in groups, many don’t want to think about life outside of University (because they are scared of it)

The change you would like to see (challenge)

Students embrace the fear of the unknown and become willing rather than reluctant.
Their research and communications show a high level of professionalism.

How you might go about bringing that change

Not really sure on the pedagogy at the moment.
Technology ~The vle we use is moodle, along with school Intranet. I also have my own webspace. We have access to laptops + ohp in the classroom.
