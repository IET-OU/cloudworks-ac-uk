HI Alisha,

I might work with any kind of special needs. We never know who's going to be in our class the following year. In my level of teaching, we have new students every year. I've been teaching children from 12 to 18 years old, but as a specialized teacher I may be working with children from 3 years old ahead.

Anyway, autism interests me a great deal so I may be interested in using your experiences, if you'd be kind enough to share. My goal is to create a course to help other teachers like me, to deal with special needs in the classroom, but I suppose I will have to specialize in a specific kind of disorder or disability, anytime now. So, it will be very useful if I come across OERs that can be used with these children.

Thank you for your kindness

Cris

Hi Cristina,

I work in a large FE/HE College designing learning materials for staff and students to use in class or online. We have a large number of students, 16-19 and adults of all ages, with learning and mobility difficulties so have experience of designing materials for that cohort.

IT skills of the teaching staff are generally not great - they use the interactive whiteboards mainly in class, not so much our VLE (virtual learning environment).

I'd be happy to get involved with you on this chatting about things that work from our perspective for students & staff.

We work with students at pre-entry level all the way up to level 1 in the Foundation Education faculty - so covers quite a wide range of abilities and needs.
