UK-wide Careers Profession Alliance - latest update (May 2011)

Dr Deirdre Hughes
14 May 2011

The UK-wide Careers Profession Alliance (CPA) came together in 2010 as a partnership of the six leading professional bodies in the field of career development[1]

Vision: One Voice – Making an Impact

‘A profession that inspires confidence in the public, dedicated to delivering high quality careers services that help individuals to achieve their full potential and support economic productivity while ensuring equality of opportunity for all.’

Its Executive Group, comprising Board/Council representatives of all six bodies, have been meeting for over a year to discuss ways of strengthening the profession, to improve services to clients/users and the professional standing of members across the whole of the UK. Part of the work being undertaken has involved looking at how the careers professional associations could be brought into a more coherent and cohesive working arrangement. Work has also been undertaken in terms of reviewing and mapping options designed to strengthen the UK careers profession for those working within and across public, private, voluntary and community settings. This includes seven key strands:

1.         Development of common professional standards

2.         A common code of ethics

3.         Development of a single career progression framework

4.         A plan for a review of initial training

5.         A plan for a review of continuous professional development (CPD)

6.         Progress to an overarching national kitemark

7.         Development of a draft business plan.

The work has built upon the earlier sector developments undertaken by Lifelong Learning UK (LLUK), the Children’s Workforce Development Council (CWDC)[2] and the Training and Development Agency for Schools (TDA)[3].

The CPA has now reached a crucial phase in its development. Each individual association must consider  and comment on the proposed development framework and, more fundamentally, how the recommendations can best be taken forward. It is intended that in June 2011, all six careers professional associations will formally consult with their members (approximately 7,700 in total) using a common set of questions based on development work undertaken between January - March 2011 and emerging key recommendations.
This is both major challange and opportunity for the UK careers profession. Three key questions:

1. Will the association Boards/Councils agree to undertake a major consultation on a proposal to work towards Chartered Status in 3-5 years?

2. Will the members welcome the proposals for a new federal arrangement, a single system  or is the current 'status quo' preferred?

3. Will young people and adults want to draw upon the expertise of careers professionals and careers educators offering high quality impartial careers information, advice and guidance and, if so, how can access, equality of opportunity and impartial provision be assured in a 'free market'?

Some form of regulation will be necessary i.e. a UK-wide register or licence to practice? The CPA has made significant progress in designing a clear 'road map' to strengthen the UK-wide careers profession. The journey is about to begin so let's hope there's sufficient energy and enthusiasm to reach our destination as a revitalised and respected profession.

Ruth Spellman,OBE, is Chair of the CPA and Chief Executive of the Chartered Management Institute (CMI).

[1] Association for Careers Education and Guidance (ACEG), Association of Graduate Careers Advisory Services (AGCAS), Association of Career Professionals International (ACPI), the Institute of Careers Guidance (ICG), National Association for Educational Guidance for Adults (NAEGA), Northern Ireland Schools and Colleges Careers Association (NISCA)

[2] England only.

[3] England and Wales only.
