Punk Academy?

  • Favourite
  • 485 views

Cloud created by:

roy williams
23 January 2013

This is a response to David's dream project: 

Hi David, I am torn between two things: 

On the one hand, I am busy trying to find ways for people to describe how they learn, so that they can reflect on and consolidate their own experience and, if they are comfortable doing so, share it with other people - of their choice.  Our work on footprints and emergence and on nested narratives goes some way to achieving that. 

On the other hand, I would like to find a way to link that into the practice of becoming a practitioner or professional of some sort. 

My gut reaction to what you are proposing is that there's a kind of 'punk-apprentice' model lurking inside there.  Mitra's SOLE/HiW kids are to some extent just hanging out, learning something that might amuse them, or make them more 'street' smart, but the more I read his reseach, the more it looks to me like they are 'punk-apprentices' of schooling (many of them, with Mitra's encouragement, aspire to get into mainstream education) or of employment (formal or informal).  

If so ... 

It might be useful to try and work-up all of this: into a 'reflective punk-apprentice' and 'reflective punk-mentor/master' model. 

It could build on some bits and pieces, such as: 

Inviting a group of potential punk-apprentices (not tooooo different, in principle, from MOOCers, really) to start using some collective tools (a 'hole in a wall' supported by a 'granny cloud' or two would be a good way to start - as a 'design-metaphor' - varied by context) - note the mix between virtual and human networks - I think both. are required. 

Add some reflective tools (e.g. narrative for the apprentices, perhaps, footprints for the mentors), and add some protocols for using those reflections - from totally private to globally public. 

Add some aggregation tools - some for the apprentices, some for the mentors, and why not throw in some for the researchers too. 

Mix to taste. 

This is a kind of a mash-up 'project', I guess, building on what you might be thinking, and what we are trying to put together. 

It is grounded, though, in 'real' apprentices, who primarily want to 'become' something, rather than want to 'learn' something, although the two obviously overlap.  A kind of 'jua kali'  virtual academy, no? Back to the future?

There is of course no reason why the learning should not be fed into an accreditation process -  at some later stage, but only if  the accreditation and assessment does not interfere with the learning.  (See benchmarking and mastery for some related ideas on this, albeit in a rather formal setting).
