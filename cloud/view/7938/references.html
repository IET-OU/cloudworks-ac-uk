Clare Gormley OLDS MOOC Week 4 Reflection: Taking stock..

  • Favourite
  • 660 views

Cloud created by:

Clare Gormley
3 February 2013

Just a quick reflection at the midpoint of this week.

I'm realising that I won't really have any time to dedicate to the MOOC this week. And given that I'm experiencing a not-so-perfect storm of masters, MOOC and money-paying work, I've decided to take a reality check on how I am likely to proceed. Quick notes below:

1. I did attend the ALT webinar "Learning design for online courses" on thursday and while the PPC looks like an interesting tool  (which I intend to play around with further), I am not going to spend time on it at the moment as it is it is unlikely to be used by the small group I am currently working with. It's a case of so many tools, so little time, I'm afraid!

2. I now realise that the time commitment involved MOOC may mean that I can only engage with the course at a somewhat superficial level for the remaining weeks. I also find that I get sucked into the vortex of the MOOC when I start working on tasks or browsing around Cloudworks. I know that this is kind of the point of this type of learning but it's very, very time-consuming (oftentimes with no "productive" output). There will have to be much more considered cherry picking ahead - the weeks on prototying, OERs and evaluation are hard to resist. Maybe I will try to in fit one 1 to 2 hour session a week? 

3. I am a bit confused about where Learning Outcomes fit into the design process? They weren't mentioned anywhere in week 3 (as far as I can see). I am presuming they need to be developed at some point, right? Might post a question on this on the discussion thread...

4. I had a good chat with fellow MOOCer Sheila and "confessed" my time commitment issues. I explained that I wasn't really going to be of much use (apart from, perhaps, as a soundboard) for a team learning design project. She was very gracious and even suggested some additional tools (eg the Viewpoints cards) that might be particularly relevant to my own work situation. Thanks Sheila! It's been so helpful to talk to someone who is familiar with (and even been involved in the development of) many of the tools we are exploring as it really gives an extra sense of which ones could have the most potential impact in a small-scale setting. And only for the MOOC, these types of conversations would never have happened.
